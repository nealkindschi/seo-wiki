# New Research: Influence Happens Everywhere

**Author:** Rand Fishkin
**Publication Date:** March 25, 2026
**Source:** SparkToro Blog

## Methodology

SparkToro partnered with Similarweb to analyze clickstream data from the top 5,000 most-visited websites. The research process involved:

- Manually categorizing the top 200 websites into 15 categories
- Using GPT for Sheets to classify the remaining 4,800 sites
- Hand-reviewing random samples across visitation tiers, achieving "99%" accuracy
- Aggregating visit data by category using January 2026 data

## Key Data Points

**Overall Traffic Distribution (Top 5,000 Sites):**
- Search and social comprise "nearly half of all visits"
- Google alone accounts for approximately 73% of search activity
- Google receives more traffic than "the next 13 largest sites by traffic combined"
- AI tools represent visits "1/1,000th the size of the breathless press coverage"

## Major Arguments

1. **Influence precedes search:** People consume content across social, news, email, and entertainment before conducting searches, meaning "Search converts interest into answers" rather than creating initial demand.
2. **Attribution bias:** Marketers over-credit search due to its "highly concentrated environment," while undervaluing fragmented channels that actually generate awareness.
3. **Social and news underutilized:** These platforms represent significant audience presence and influence but remain "most-overlooked channels."
4. **Google's AI dominance matters most:** Rather than standalone AI tools, Google's integration—including AI overviews in search results—poses the greatest competitive challenge.

## Noted Limitations

- Mobile app data excluded (social, email, messaging would appear larger if included)
- AI usage appears inflated due to desktop skew
- Gmail redirects to Google.com, undercounting email visits
- Malware visits included but noted as non-influential for marketing
