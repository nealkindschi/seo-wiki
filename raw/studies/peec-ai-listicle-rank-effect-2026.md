# The Listicle Rank Effect: What Nearly 200,000 AI Responses Across 8 AI Engines Reveal About Brand Visibility

Source: https://peec.ai/blog/the-listicle-rank-effect-what-nearly-200-000-ai-responses-across-8-ai-engines-reveal-about-brand-visibility
Author: Jan Ehrlinspiel (GEO Researcher, Peec AI)
Published: 2026-05-14
Fetched: 2026-07-08

## Methodology

Nearly 200,000 AI responses analyzed, 5.7M+ individual data points.
8 engines: ChatGPT, Claude Sonnet 4, Gemini, Google AI Mode, Google AI
Overview, GPT-5 Search, Microsoft Copilot, Perplexity. Study period
September 2025-March 2026. Three markets analyzed: B2B SaaS, Emerging
MarTech, US Finance.

## Core Argument

Brands appearing in frequently-cited third-party listicles show
significantly higher AI-response visibility. Rank position within a
listicle matters, but its effect size and shape depend on market
maturity/structure, not a single universal curve.

## Finding 1: Rank effect varies by market

- Emerging MarTech (sharpest rank effect): rank #1 gives a +13.4
  percentage-point visibility lift, with a sharp dropoff at lower
  positions.
- B2B SaaS (flatter impact): rank #1 gives a +16.5pp lift, but
  established brands already included show less rank sensitivity.
- US Finance (trusted-source effect): a smaller listicle pool produces
  large overall effects but a less predictable rank ordering.

## Finding 2: Rank shapes answer position

Estimated improvement in answer placement for a rank-#1 listicle
appearance: US Finance 1.80 positions earlier, B2B SaaS 1.17 positions
earlier, Emerging MarTech 0.82 positions earlier — directionally
consistent across all three industries.

## Finding 3: Mention count diverges by market

Estimated mention-count increase from rank-#1 exposure: Emerging
MarTech +0.67, US Finance +0.66 (not statistically significant), B2B
SaaS +0.43.

## Finding 4: Diminishing returns from multiple listicles

Maximum impact is typically reached after just a few repeated
placements. Five strong placements in frequently-cited sources
outperform fifty placements in rarely-retrieved articles.

## Finding 5: Engine-specific behavior

Tight-retrieval engines (ChatGPT, GPT-5 Search, Microsoft Copilot) show
larger visibility associations for top-ranked listicles. Broad-retrieval
engines (Google AI Overview, Google AI Mode) show smaller individual
lifts per listicle.

## Strategic Recommendations

Identify the specific listicles AI engines actually cite repeatedly for
commercially important prompts, then focus on improving rank/placement
within those sources rather than pursuing broad listicle coverage.
Fragmented/emerging markets warrant immediate placement focus;
established markets benefit from broader brand-building alongside
listicle optimization.
