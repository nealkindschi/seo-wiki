# Semrush AI Overviews Study: What 2025 SEO Data Tells Us About Google's Search Shift

**Original Publication:** March 2025
**Updated:** December 15, 2025
**Author:** Jana Garanko
**Contributors:** Luke Harsel, Anna Yudina, Aleksandr Drozdov, Christine Skopec

## Methodology

- 10M+ keywords tracking AI Overview prevalence and monthly changes by intent type
- 10K keywords examining length, difficulty, search volume, and SERP feature co-occurrence
- 200K+ keywords for zero-click rate trends (January-October 2025)
- 11K domains to assess visibility impact by industry
- Partnership with Datos for clickstream analysis

## Key Findings

### AI Overview Prevalence
- Started 2025 at 6.49% of queries
- Peaked at 24.61% in July
- Settled at 15.69% in November
- Overall growth of 155% comparing Q4 to Q1

### Search Intent Shifts
- Commercial queries: up from 8.15% to 18.57%
- Transactional queries: up from 1.98% to 13.94%
- Navigational queries: up from 0.84% to 10.33%

### Zero-Click Behavior
The zero-click rate decreased from 33.75% to 31.53% when tracking keywords before/after AI Overview introduction — suggesting the feature doesn't automatically eliminate clicks.

### Industry Impact
**Most Saturated:** Science (25.96%), Computers & Electronics (17.92%), People & Society (17.29%)
**Fastest Growth:** Food & Drink (+7.25% since March)
**Least Impacted:** Real Estate, Shopping, Arts & Entertainment (<3%)

### Query Characteristics
Keywords triggering AI Overviews tend to be longer and more specific, with nearly 60% having 100 or fewer monthly searches and 60% falling into the 21-60 keyword difficulty range.

### SERP Feature Changes
- Featured snippets, image carousels, organic reviews: decreased co-occurrence
- Related searches (95.32%) and People Also Ask (90.03%): nearly guaranteed presence
- Google Ads: dramatic increase from <1% in March to 25% by November

## Key Takeaway
"SEO becomes more about presence than position" as Google transforms into an answer engine rather than purely a search engine.
