# AI Overviews are expanding across commercial intent search (Semrush)

**Publication Date:** July 2, 2026
**Author:** Luke Harsel
**Contributors:** Anna Yudina, Christine Skopec

## Methodology

600,000+ keywords from Semrush's US desktop database, November 2025 to
April 2026. Keywords segmented by industry (10 total), search intent
category, and CPC level. Measured monthly AI Overview keyword shares,
coexistence frequency with Google Ads on SERPs, and variation across
industries/intent categories.

## Key Findings

### Commercial vs. Transactional Intent
- Commercial-intent AI Overviews grew 71% during the study period.
- Transactional-intent AI Overviews decreased 5%.
- Finance showed the largest commercial growth (+231%), followed by
  Computers & Electronics (+107.62%) and Games (+76.66%).

### Google Ads and AI Overviews Coexistence
- SERPs displaying both Google Ads and AI Overviews now appear roughly
  2x more frequently than a year prior — a shift from AIOs being
  largely limited to low-intent informational queries.

### High-CPC Keywords
- AI Overviews appeared most frequently in the highest CPC tier across
  nearly every industry.
- Jobs & Education: over 60% of expensive keywords trigger an AI
  Overview.
- Average CPC with vs. without AI Overview: Jobs & Education $5.02 vs.
  $1.51; Finance $4.84 vs. $2.14.

### Industry-Specific Patterns
- Largest commercial-vs-transactional AIO gaps: Internet & Telecom,
  Finance, Travel.
- Inverted gap: Food & Drink showed 16% transactional vs. 14%
  commercial AI Overviews.
- Lower-CPC industries (Games, Internet & Telecom, Hobbies & Leisure)
  showed opposite CPC patterns.

## Strategic Implications

Marketers must compete across multiple SERP positions simultaneously:
within the AI Overview summary, among its citation sources, in organic
rankings, and in paid placements. Longer research-phase industries
(Finance, Travel) show greater AI Overview integration than
quick-decision categories (Food & Drink).
