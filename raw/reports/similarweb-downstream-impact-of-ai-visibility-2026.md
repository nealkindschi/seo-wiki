# The Downstream Impact of AI Visibility (Similarweb)

**Publication Date:** June 24, 2026
**Publisher:** Similarweb

First study connecting AI *visibility* (being recommended by ChatGPT)
to downstream traffic/business impact by following real user journeys.

## Methodology

- Data source: Similarweb's opted-in U.S. desktop web panel
- Time period: July through December 2025
- Industries studied: Finance, Travel, Beauty — competitive brand pairs
  with topical overlap
- Tracking method: user journeys from a ChatGPT recommendation to a
  website visit within seven days
- Brand pairs: American Express vs. Capital One (Finance), Skyscanner
  vs. Kayak (Travel), Sephora vs. Ulta (Beauty)

## Key Findings

**Traffic impact**: brands recommended by ChatGPT are 2.5x more likely,
on average, to receive a visit within the following seven days compared
to a non-recommended direct competitor — this multiplier held across
all three industries studied.

**Industry-specific visit rates**:
- Finance: American Express 7.2% post-recommendation visit rate vs.
  Capital One 3.1%
- Travel: Skyscanner 9.5% vs. Kayak 7.6%
- Beauty: Sephora 7.9% vs. Ulta 3.3%

**How the traffic actually arrives**: 55.9% of AI-influenced traffic
arrives via search, not as a direct referral from ChatGPT. A user gets
a brand recommendation from ChatGPT, then searches for the brand name in
a search engine; standard analytics dashboards see this as ordinary
branded organic search, even though the AI recommendation happened
first and caused the search. Direct traffic accounts for only 19.9% of
AI-influenced visits.

**Engagement quality**: AI-influenced visitors engaged much harder —
12.0 pages and 11.8 minutes per session, versus 6.5 pages and 5.6
minutes for non-AI-influenced visitors.

## Caveats (stated by Similarweb)

- US desktop only — explicitly excludes mobile and international
  traffic.
- Rests on three verticals and a set of named brand pairs, not a
  whole-web sample.
- Explicitly framed as correlational, not proven causal.
