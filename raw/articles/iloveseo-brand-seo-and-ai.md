# The Role of SEO in Making Branding Understood by Search Engines and AI

Source: https://www.iloveseo.net/the-role-of-seo-in-making-branding-understood-by-search-engines-and-ai/
Author: Gianluca Fiorelli
Published: 2024-12-11

## Core Premise
Google and AI systems don't inherently "understand" brands — brands must be translated into machine-readable signals through SEO. Brand = "a distinctive identity that includes the name, symbol, design, values and reputation associated with a product, service or organization, designed to differentiate it in the market and create a psychological and emotional bond with consumers." For machines, this becomes: a named entity with distinctive characteristics (name/logo/design), clear values (entity attributes), external trust signals, authority signals, and associations with other named entities through branded queries.

## Trial 1: Knowledge Graph & Structured Data
- Audit Knowledge Graph presence (Serpapi, Audits.com)
- Implement comprehensive Organization schema (40+ properties available)
- Prioritize `sameAs` for linking to business profiles
- Use hierarchical markup (`parentOrganization`, `subOrganization`) for complex corporate structures
- Use `about` and `mention` properties to clarify entity relationships
- Key insight: "Structured data must be considered as the semantic mapping of all the relationships existing between the entities of our website."
- Knowledge Graph data sources: Google's own ecosystem (Business, Merchant Center, YouTube), social networks (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn, X), trusted seeds (Amazon, IMDB, Amazon Music), secondary sources (Crunchbase, Glassdoor).

## Trial 2: Branded Query Analysis
- Monitor branded searches via Google Search Console
- Analyze SERP features for branded queries to find opportunities
- Study competitor branded queries to predict market patterns
- Include product-specific branded searches (e.g. "Nike Jordan 1")
- Consult Google Trends and People Also Ask
- Example: Games Workshop's Italian branded searches heavily featured "Middle Earth Strategy Battle Game," suggesting menu restructuring and product-prominence optimization.

## Trial 3: Branded Awareness & Content Strategy
"Winning zone" framework: content at the intersection of buyer personas (target audience), audience personas (influencers/amplifiers), and business objectives.
- Content must satisfy user intent AND a beneficial purpose
- Informational yet actionable
- Distinctive value, not generic/replaceable
- Evergreen over decaying blog posts
- Should naturally attract backlinks, mentions, co-citations

Case studies:
- **Asics Running Guide**: animated, educational content addressing runner pain points, includes purchase CTAs, generates sustained backlink profile over time.
- **Idealista Real Estate Reports**: data-driven market analysis, became authoritative reference for journalists/professionals, generates high-authority backlinks naturally, synonymous with "real estate" positioning.

## Trial 4: Repurpose & Amplification
- Coordinate with marketing across channels (social, paid, content marketing)
- Share across owned and influencer channels
- Create a "virtuous flywheel" via mentions, co-citations, backlinks
- Examples: Spotify Wrapped creates December spikes in branded search; Games Workshop YouTube announcements drive branded search volume (2024 Age of Sigmar launch).

## The Ordeal: Organizational Challenges
- SEO professionals often work in silos, treating SEO as hierarchically superior to other marketing disciplines — causes collaboration failures.
- Mindset shift: study the brand's own documentation ("Brand Bible") before strategy; align with overall brand voice/identity; prioritize collaboration over technical isolation; frame SEO through business outcomes, not vanity metrics.
- References the "Product Network Triangle": balance of business goals, user needs, technology capabilities — SEO operates at the User-Business intersection.

## Statistics Cited
- 44.19% of searches are brand-related (Sparktoro/Rand Fishkin)
- Navboost algorithm uses SERP engagement signals (CTR) for initial result filtering
- Documented "brand bias" — people instinctively prefer recognized brands

## LLM-Specific Considerations
AI Overview and LLM systems (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) similarly weight user engagement patterns, entity associations, co-occurrences/co-citations, and **monosemanticity** — the clarity of word/concept alignment within content context.

## Practical Checklist
1. Audit Knowledge Graph presence/completeness
2. Implement comprehensive structured data across entity types
3. Monitor/analyze branded query patterns
4. Map content to the "winning zone"
5. Build evergreen content assets vs. ephemeral blog posts
6. Coordinate cross-channel amplification
7. Establish internal collaboration protocols with dev/marketing
8. Frame ROI in business terms, not ranking metrics
