# Why Calling Yourself The Best Could Be Helping Your Competitors Win In AI Search

Source: https://www.searchenginejournal.com/why-calling-yourself-the-best-could-be-helping-your-competitors-win-in-ai-search/579778/
Author: Lily Ray (Founder, Algorythmic)
Published: 2026-06-18
Fetched: 2026-07-08

## Core Thesis

Google's AI Overviews have decoupled *citation* from *recommendation*.
A brand that publishes a self-ranked "best of" listicle (naming itself
#1) can still get that listicle cited as a source, while the AI
Overview's actual recommendation goes to a competitor named within
that same listicle — effectively using the self-promoter's own content
to promote rivals.

## Headline Statistics

- Self-promotional listicles were cited as a source but the publishing
  brand was excluded from the AI's actual recommendation in 224 of 323
  tracked instances — a 69% failure rate.
- Across 100 B2B queries analyzed, roughly 74% returned an AI Overview
  that cited a self-promoter's listicle while omitting that brand from
  the recommendation itself.
- Sites relying on this tactic saw organic-visibility declines
  beginning January 2026, accelerating through May 2026.

## Citation vs. Recommendation Mechanism

Demonstrated across multiple named examples (Oasis LMS, Kredo
Learning, Pylon, TMetric, Pollfish): when a brand's own "best X"
listicle gets cited as an AI Overview source, the AI frequently uses
it to recommend one of the *competitors* named within that listicle
rather than the listicle's publisher/self-promoter.

## Authority Moderates the Effect

Domain Rating, backlink volume, and existing AI-mention frequency
analysis suggests already-established, high-authority brands are
sometimes exempted from this exclusion pattern — the citation-without-
recommendation penalty falls disproportionately on lesser-known
companies, even when those companies rank well organically for the
same query.

## Reported Google Countermeasures

1. Organic ranking demotion targeting domains with a pattern of
   excessive self-promotional content.
2. New AI Overview disclaimer language flagging "self-proclaimed
   expert" sources and prompting users to independently evaluate
   claims.
3. A citation-source shift toward third-party review platforms
   (Reddit, Forbes Advisor, YouTube) specifically for "best of"-style
   queries.

## Trend Context

Self-promotional listicles surged through 2025: 184 such listicles
identified from 146 distinct brands. Early adopters gained a
competitive advantage before the tactic became common; broad adoption
appears to have triggered the algorithmic correction described above.

## Strategic Takeaways

Don't publish self-promotional "best of" listicles expecting an AI
recommendation, especially without pre-existing authority signals.
Instead, build genuine third-party endorsements and earn mentions
across the broader web — that's what actually drives AI
recommendations, not self-citation. Cites Pew Research's finding that
AI-summary citations produce only about a 1% click-through rate,
reinforcing that *citation alone* has limited direct business value —
recommendation is the metric that actually matters.
