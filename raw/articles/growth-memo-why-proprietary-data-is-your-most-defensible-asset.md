# Why proprietary data is your most defensible AI citation asset

**Title:** Why proprietary data is your most defensible AI citation asset
**Publication Date:** June 29, 2026
**Authors:** Kevin Indig and Amanda Johnson

## Key Arguments

**Original Data as a Competitive Advantage**
The authors argue that publishing original data represents the strongest predictor of page originality. Research from On-Page.ai showed pages with 15+ unique figures averaged an information gain score of 62.1, compared to 40.2 for pages with minimal original data. The authors note "Most pages are middling on originality" and standing out requires genuine differentiation rather than exceptional effort.

**Ownership Doesn't Guarantee Citations**
A critical finding: possessing proprietary data doesn't automatically result in AI citations. Aggregators can repackage research into cleaner formats and capture citations instead. The study identified that "44.2% of all citations come from the first 30% of a page," indicating placement matters significantly.

**Structural Requirements for Extraction**
To win AI citations, content must be structured for machine extraction:
- Lead with headline statistics in the first 30% of content
- Define metrics immediately with context
- Box methodology information separately
- Front-load secondary findings by strength
- Eliminate suspenseful narrative structures

**Data Sources**
Most businesses generate unused proprietary data—product usage, pricing, customer behavior, and performance metrics—that can be published without dedicated research budgets.
