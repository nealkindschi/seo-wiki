# Why Most Original Data Never Gets Cited

Source: https://www.growth-memo.com/p/why-most-original-data-never-gets
Authors: Kevin Indig and Amanda Johnson
Platform: Growth Memo (Substack)
Published: 2026-07-06
Fetched: 2026-07-07

## Core Argument

Creating original data is valuable for earning AI citations, but format
and presentation matter more than the data itself. Simply publishing
proprietary numbers rarely generates citations unless the content takes
a specific form: a benchmark that answers "which option is best?" on
measurable criteria.

## Methodology

Analyzed Gauge's citation dataset:
- 301 live pages cited by AI systems.
- 316 unique prompts across 7 verticals.
- 1,075 total citations distributed among these pages.
- Full URL audits to identify primary research sources.
- Analysis of citation distribution patterns and clustering.

## Key Findings

**Rarity and density:**
- Only 8 of 301 pages (2.7%) qualified as primary research.
- Those 8 pages earned 90 of 1,075 citations (8.4%).
- Primary research pages averaged 11.3 citations vs. 3.4 for others —
  "3.3x as citation-dense."

**Benchmark dominance:**
- 75 of 90 primary-research citations came from cloud data warehouse
  benchmarks.
- Fivetran's warehouse benchmark alone captured 44 citations (nearly
  half of all primary-research citations).
- Fivetran's two benchmark pages together = 58 of 90 primary citations.
- Benchmark clustering appears in topics requiring measurable
  comparisons (speed, cost, latency, performance).

**Topic variation:**
- Crypto/Solana showed similar patterns at smaller scale.
- Topics without clear comparison frameworks (B2B SaaS/CRM,
  Education/TEFL, Product Analytics) produced no cited primary
  research at all.

## Key Definitions

- **Primary Research**: content where the original data source and
  methodology appear on the page itself, rather than referencing
  another source's numbers.
- **Benchmark**: content measuring a set of named items against each
  other on specific metrics, published as comparable results.
- **Citation-Dense**: pages earning disproportionate citation volume
  relative to their prevalence in the overall content set.

## Why Fivetran's benchmark keeps earning citations (2022 content)

Seven characteristics identified:
1. **Direct comparison framing** — named warehouses (BigQuery, Redshift,
   Snowflake, Databricks) ranked on speed and cost.
2. **First-party testing data** — real customer usage, not synthetic
   assumptions, explicitly acknowledged as such.
3. **Transparent methodology** — step-by-step documentation of queries,
   configuration, and tuning.
4. **AI-retrievable structure** — descriptive headings mapping questions
   to answers.
5. **Source transparency** — linked raw data and references with
   verifiable claims.
6. **Credibility signals** — dated corrections, qualitative limits,
   acknowledged caveats.
7. **URL stability** — a single canonical address maintained since 2022.

Quote: "The data behind a page like this is easier to pull and analyze
than it has ever been. What is not easy is everything around it: the
clean method, the linked sources, the corrections, the navigable
structure."

## Recommendations

**Citation-ready research structure:**
1. Lead with the comparison result in the first 30% of content.
2. Box the methodology: sample, timeframe, measurement approach,
   attribution confidence.
3. Explicitly frame as a comparison, with tables comparing named
   options.
4. Maintain URL stability — 64 of 365 cited URLs in the dataset were
   dead or redirected, taking 203 citations offline.

**Strategic positioning:** an "open opportunity" exists to publish
retrievable datasets for buyer questions that currently lack clean
benchmark sources. Best-positioned: companies sitting on proprietary
product, usage, or pricing data who package it as actionable buyer
comparisons.

**Why most fail:** brands often publish proprietary numbers but bury
them in narrative, gate them behind forms, change URLs, or omit
methodology. The data exists but lacks citation-ready packaging.

## Example

HockeyStack published 18 original reports using anonymized first-party
customer data, treating methodology as non-negotiable and structuring
reports around specific ICP questions.

## Conclusion

AI systems reward not original data broadly, but "first-party research
when the page gives it a clean answer to a measurable comparison that's
built to signal depth of expertise and trust." The competitive
advantage lies in packaging proprietary information into stable,
methodology-transparent benchmarks addressing specific commercial
comparisons — work requiring substantially more effort than data
collection itself.
