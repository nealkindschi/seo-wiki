# SaaS SEO: The Ultimate Guide

Source: https://gofishdigital.com/blog/seo-for-saas/
Author: Chris Long, VP of Marketing at Go Fish Digital
Published: 2023-09-10

## Core Definition
Improving the organic visibility of SaaS websites via landing pages, content integration, and blog strategy.

## Key SaaS-Specific Challenges
- **Aggregator competition**: Capterra dominates SaaS search with 750+ category pages; users prefer comparing multiple solutions at once, making single-vendor pages hard to rank against comparison platforms.
- **Sales cycle length**: SaaS averages an 84.3-day sales cycle — requires optimizing awareness, consideration, and purchase stages, not just product pages.
- **Cross-team conflicts**: SEO and product teams often disagree on terminology/positioning; requires clear communication.

## Keyword Research Tactics
1. **Capterra analysis**: mine competitor Capterra profiles for category keywords and "Featured In" sections.
2. **Google Ads data**: prioritize keywords driving conversions beyond trial signup (demos, calls, subscribes).
3. **Competitor blog research** (Ahrefs): identify long-tail keywords where aggregators lack presence.
4. **Reverse gap analysis**: find keywords where competitors rank but Capterra doesn't.

## Landing Page Strategy
- **Product pages**: CTAs, demo videos, "What Is [Solution]" sections, case-study links, FAQ sections.
- **Features pages**: individual pages per feature targeting long-tail searches.
- **Industries pages**: vertical-specific solutions (example: "church text" achieved a featured snippet).
- **Alternatives pages**: competitor comparisons; one client captured a featured snippet for alternative-seeking users.

## On-Page Optimization
- Use "software" terminology despite brand resistance, when search volume justifies it.
- Link landing pages to case studies, videos, testimonials, blogs (content integration).
- Target featured snippets (paragraph and list formats) with consistent formatting.

## Blog Strategy
Quote: "This is probably the number one area I see people wasting time, resources, cash is trying to get their product pages to rank that nobody wants a single vendor product page to rank for." Recommendation: build blog content for informational queries where aggregators have gaps (e.g. "create legal timeline," "email marketing software").

## Link Building
"Tangential content" approach — data studies, maps, surveys on adjacent topics to earn PR coverage/backlinks. Case example: "Remote Working Statistics" generated 95 backlinks from 69 domains.

## Statistics/Case Studies
- SMS marketing software client: +41% lift in first-page keyword rankings since July 2018
- HubSpot's email marketing article ranks #6, ahead of MailChimp's product page
- Search volume examples: "Node.js logging" (300-400 MSV), "church text" (4,400 MSV)

## Frameworks
- Featured snippet optimization: consistent, concise answers positioned optimally (paragraph/list format).
- Content gap analysis: compare competitor rankings where Capterra is absent to find lower-competition opportunities.
