# The Consensus Gap

Source: https://www.searchenginejournal.com/the-consensus-gap/574534/
Author: Kevin Indig (VIP Contributor, Growth Advisor)
Published: 2026-05-12
Fetched: 2026-07-07

## Core Argument

Challenges treating "AI visibility" as one unified metric. Visibility
operates as three separate distribution systems with minimal overlap.
A blended AEO score "hides the only finding that matters."

## Key Findings & Data

- Only **2.35-2.45%** of cited URLs appeared in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and
  Google AI Overviews for the *same prompt*.
- **91%** of citations appeared in only one engine.
- The ~2% overlap held nearly flat across four separate samples spanning
  Q3 2025 through Q1 2026.
- Commercial queries: 2.4% universal overlap. Informational queries:
  2.0%.

**Performance by content type (cross-engine overlap):**
- Guides/tutorials: 2.3% (highest)
- Blogs: 1.8%
- Category pages: 1.6%
- Product pages: 1.2%
- Homepages: 1.1%

**Domain-specific examples:**
- Wikipedia: 16,073 citations total, only 1.3% appeared universally.
- Reddit: 14,267 citations, only 0.1% universal overlap.
- Reuters: 1,202 citations, 0.0% universal overlap.

## Key Definitions

- **Presence**: % of tracked prompts where a domain appears in *any*
  engine — whether visibility exists at all.
- **Portability**: % of cited URLs appearing in *all three* engines —
  whether visibility is resilient across platforms.
- **Concentration**: % of citations from a single engine — which
  platform dominates a brand's dashboard.
- **The Consensus Gap**: the structural fragmentation where engines draw
  "from largely disjoint pools rather than ranking the same pool
  differently."

## Methodology

- 3.7 million total URL citations analyzed.
- Four prompt samples: three cohorts of 5,000 prompts each (tracked
  Jan 1 2025, Jul 1 2025, Jan 1 2026), plus a separate 20,000-prompt
  random sample.
- 2.6M URLs for commercial/informational/other intent splits; 4.1M URL
  appearances for page-type analysis.
- Engines: ChatGPT (default production model, logged-out web browsing),
  Perplexity (Sonar), Google AI Overviews (default production model).
  Prompts fired concurrently within the same minute, twice daily, with
  country localization.
- Scope skew: Omnia's customer base (Spain-heavy + UK/Nordic/EU),
  primary-country-language queries, fintech/insurtech/travel/SaaS/B2B
  focus. Intent/page-type classification via regex (directional, not
  authoritative).

## Strategic Recommendations

1. Stop treating AI visibility as one metric — measure presence,
   portability, and concentration separately instead of a blended AEO
   score.
2. Prioritize explanatory content (guides/tutorials) — it travels best
   across engines, though absolute portability is still low.
3. Question which single engine matters most for your business rather
   than pursuing generic cross-engine visibility.
4. Shift measurement focus from presence to portability — build "assets
   that survive different engine preferences" rather than assuming one
   optimization approach works universally.
5. Reframe the diagnostic question from "How do we rank in AI?" to "How
   do we build assets that work across engine preferences?" — engines
   "favor utility over brand centrality."

## Conclusions

A fundamentally fragmented AI citation landscape: engines maintain
largely separate source pools, making aggregated visibility metrics
misleading. Even dominant domains like Wikipedia have minimal
cross-platform portability. Content type matters less than expected,
but explanatory formats outperform transactional/brand-centric pages.
Teams should abandon single-leaderboard thinking, build engine-specific
strategies, and develop content resilient enough to travel across
different retrieval logics.
