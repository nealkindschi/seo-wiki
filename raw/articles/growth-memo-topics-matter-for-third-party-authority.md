# Topics Matter for Third-Party Authority Signals

**Title:** "Topics Matter for Third-Party Authority Signals"
**Publication Date:** June 15, 2026
**Authors:** Kevin Indig and Amanda Johnson
**Publication:** Growth Memo (Substack)

## Key Arguments

**1. AI Uses Topic-Specific Source Sets**
Different AI search engines rely on different trusted sources depending on the query topic. For invoicing questions, competitor domains represent 33.5% of citations, while for business startup questions, that figure drops to 7%. This means authority-building strategies must be tailored by topic rather than applied generically.

**2. Authority Pays in Steps, Not Linearly**
Research analyzing 1,000 domains found that Authority Score shows the strongest correlation (0.65 Pearson) with AI mentions. Rather than accumulating evenly, citation gains occur in tiers — three placements in a top-decile source outperform a dozen mentions across low-authority sites.

**3. Named Authors Outperform Brand Accounts**
"Fresh, expert-authored, clearly time-stamped content earned the fastest visibility" according to LinkedIn's analysis. Individual bylines with established credibility generate stronger signals than faceless brand publications.

**4. Recommended Action Steps**
The article recommends: identifying 2-3 credible subject matter experts, mapping which sources AI already cites, concentrating efforts on high-authority publications, pursuing nofollow opportunities, creating embeddable data assets, and leveraging LinkedIn for rapid indexing.
