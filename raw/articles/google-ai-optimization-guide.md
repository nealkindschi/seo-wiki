# Google's Guide to Optimizing for Generative AI Features on Google Search

Source: https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/ai-optimization-guide
Last updated (per page): 2026-06-29 UTC
Fetched: 2026-07-07

## Is SEO still relevant?

"In short, yes! The best practices for SEO continue to be relevant
because our generative AI features on Google Search are rooted in our
core Search ranking and quality systems." Google's AI features depend on
core search ranking systems using techniques like "retrieval-augmented
generation (RAG)" and "query fan-out" to retrieve and present indexed
content with clickable links.

## Core recommendations

1. **Create valuable, non-commodity content**
   - Develop unique viewpoints rather than recycling existing information
   - Write "helpful, reliable, and people-first" content with genuine expertise
   - Organize clearly with headings and sections
   - Include quality images and videos where relevant
   - Avoid creating excessive page variations solely to manipulate rankings

2. **Build clear technical structure**
   - Meet basic search technical requirements for indexing eligibility
   - Ensure content remains crawlable and publicly accessible
   - Follow JavaScript SEO best practices when applicable
   - Provide good page experience across devices
   - Reduce duplicate content

3. **Optimize business details**
   - Use Google Merchant Center feeds for product visibility
   - Maintain Google Business Profiles for local information

## What NOT to do

Google explicitly states these approaches won't help:
- Creating "llms.txt" files or special AI markup: "You don't need to
  create new machine readable files, AI text files, markup, or Markdown
  to appear in Google Search (including its generative AI capabilities),
  as Google Search itself doesn't use them."
- "Chunking" content into tiny pieces: "There's no requirement to break
  your content into tiny pieces for AI to better understand it. Google
  systems are able to understand the nuance of multiple topics on a page
  and show the relevant piece to users."
- Rewriting specifically for AI systems
- Pursuing inauthentic mentions or backlinks
- Obsessing over structured data: "Structured data isn't required for
  generative AI search, and there's no special schema.org markup you need
  to add. However, it's a good idea to continue using it as part of your
  overall SEO strategy."

## Bottom line

The page emphasizes that traditional SEO fundamentals remain the
foundation for generative AI visibility in Google Search.
