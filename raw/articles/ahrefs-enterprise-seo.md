# Enterprise SEO Strategies For Maximum Growth

Source: https://ahrefs.com/blog/enterprise-seo/
Author: Patrick Stox (reviewed by Joshua Hardwick)
Published/updated: 2025-01-21

## Core Definition
Enterprise SEO = optimizing large-scale company websites, identified by company size, tech/team complexity, global operations, or multi-location structure. Distinction from standard SEO: scale, complexity, competition level.

## Benefits
Credibility, growth (visibility/awareness), revenue (conversions, lower CAC), channel support (retargeting via paid/content).

## Content Marketing Strategy
- Start with competitor top-page analysis, not keyword lists
- Prioritize informational content + video initially for ROI; later expand to events, courses, case studies, white papers, podcasts
- Top-of-funnel content expands pipeline/thought leadership even though bottom-of-funnel converts
- Featured snippet optimization: target positions #1-5 with an existing snippet where you don't rank
- Content improvement priorities: declining-traffic pages, low-hanging fruit (positions 4-15), featured-snippet targets
- Translate successful content for international markets
- Don't keyword-stuff branded/product pages to rank for informational terms
- Tactics: syndicate successful content; redirect old URLs with backlinks to current pages; build internal links via topic relationships; create "vs" comparison pages; develop free tools as lead-gen; build programmatic content ("SEO for X" pages); produce intent-structured video content

## Link Building Strategy
- Create linkable assets: surveys/studies, tools, awards, how-to guides, infographics
- Leverage high-performing pages — well-ranked content attracts more links over time ("vicious circle of SEO")
- Consolidate cannibalized/duplicate content into comprehensive guides, redirect weaker pages
- Amplify via PR, social, paid media, influencers
- Recover unlinked mentions via Content Explorer; request linkage
- Link reclamation: redirect 404s with existing backlinks — ~two-thirds of links disappear over 9 years
- Analyze competitor link sources ("Best by links" reports)
- Cross-domain linking between company-owned sites
- M&A: acquire content and links through acquisitions

## Technical SEO Strategy
**High priority**: indexation check (noindex/canonicalization), link recovery via redirects, internal-link opportunities from pages discussing ranked topics, schema implementations that earn search features, page experience (Core Web Vitals, HTTPS, mobile-friendliness, intrusive interstitials).
**Medium priority**: general maintenance (broken links, redirect chains >5 hops), automated sitemaps.
**Specialized**: hreflang (international), crawl budget (millions of pages), ecommerce (faceted nav, query params, pagination, JS rendering), migrations (minimize-impact standards), M&A traffic integration, log file analysis (less critical than previously thought).

## Local SEO (Enterprise)
Multi-location optimization, bulk Google Business Profile verification, prioritize high-performing/underperforming locations. Stat: 78% of people who search for something nearby on their phones visit the business within a day.

## SaaS & Ecommerce
- SaaS: attract searchers seeking the solution your product provides
- Ecommerce PDPs: don't rewrite manufacturer descriptions verbatim — add value via reviews, comparisons, custom attributes
- Ecommerce PLPs/categories: show diverse products across important facets, quality over quantity

## Organizational Challenges

### Getting Buy-In
- Quantify search volume for key terms
- Stat: 90.63% of pages get no organic search traffic from Google
- Stat: average top-ranking page ranks in top 10 for ~1,000 other keywords
- Include company-specific metrics: organic traffic %, conversions, revenue, YoY growth
- Repeat messaging consistently; tie everything to revenue

### Resource Planning & Prioritization
- Impact-Effort Matrix: quick wins (high-impact, low-effort) first
- Align projects with company goals; create yearly/quarterly SEO forecasts
- Avoid "busy work" — focus macro-level
- Mix company-wide, team-specific, and individual project types
- Accept enterprise SEO moves slower than agency SEO: "Boring = $$$"

### Reporting & Data
- Adjust content for audience (executives want revenue, teams want KPIs)
- Higher levels prefer one consolidated metric; use visualizations over raw numbers
- Compare against competitors; share data cross-departmentally (share-of-voice for execs, keyword data for paid/product teams)
- Use storytelling; celebrate team wins

### Organizational Structure
- SEO teams sit in varied departments (marketing, analytics, product, dev, content)
- Specialize by discipline: content, links, technical
- Collaborate across taxonomy, events, branding, legal, PR teams
- Build visibility: weekly meetings, office hours, lunch-and-learns
- Be opportunistic — join projects when teams are ready
- External agencies fill gaps, provide second opinions, reinforce in-house perspective

### Training & Evangelism
- Team trainings, workshops, internal courses
- Insert SEO into company events; internal newsletters with industry updates
- Build internal evangelists

## Process Frameworks

### Content Development Process
1. Gather search-query/competitor-coverage data
2. Card-sorting exercise: place research findings on cards
3. Writers organize cards into topics/subtopics
4. Provide outline/content brief
5. Create author portfolios to track performance/identify top performers

### Redirect Matching Process
Automated script pulling Ahrefs API + analytics data to flag redirect candidates. Criteria: >3 referring domains, >5 hits/month, etc. Track already-redirected pages for consolidated value after 1+ year.

### Content Gap Analysis Process
1. Export competitor top pages/keywords
2. Combine data to find working content
3. Use Content Gap tool or Keywords Explorer
4. Cluster keywords by parent topic to reduce noise
5. Identify true opportunities beyond similar-keyword noise

## Statistics
- 90.63% of pages get zero organic traffic from Google
- Average top-ranking page ranks top-10 for ~1,000 other keywords
- ~2/3 of links disappear over a 9-year period
- 30% of mobile searches are location-related; 78% of nearby-search visitors visit within 24 hours; 28% of nearby searches result in purchase
- Amazon.com: 275M ranking pages, 686M monthly organic visits; Microsoft.com: 516M monthly organic visits
- 44% of S&P 500 use Ahrefs for SEO tools
- Enterprise SEO consulting: ~$5,000-$50,000+/month

## Tools
Ahrefs (Site Explorer, Keywords Explorer, Content Explorer, Site Audit, Rank Tracker, Webmaster Tools), Google Search Console, PageSpeed Insights, Google Analytics; alternatives: Conductor, seoClarity, Botify, BrightEdge.
