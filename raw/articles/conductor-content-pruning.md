# Content Pruning

Source: https://www.conductor.com/academy/content-pruning/
Author: Steven van Vessum (VP of Organic Marketing, FamilyCare)
Last updated: 2026-03-24

## Definition
Content pruning removes obsolete and low-performing content that diminishes website quality — like pruning a tree so energy focuses on growth areas.

## Why Prune
- Overall quality improvement (removes non-value-adding material)
- Better link authority distribution (backlinks flow to high-potential pages)
- Enhanced user experience (current, relevant information)
- Optimized crawl budget (especially for 10,000+ page sites)

## When to Prune
Continuous, staged pruning rather than annual overhauls:
- Sites up to 1,000 pages: every 6 months
- Larger sites: every 3 months
- Monthly assessment as part of regular content work

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Content Inventory
Complete list of URLs/pages/images/videos/PDFs from CMS exports, web analytics, GSC/Bing Webmaster Tools, backlink data (e.g. Ahrefs). Document goals, target audience, target keywords per item.

### Step 2: Content Audit
Evaluate: general traffic/conversions (12 months), organic traffic specifically, internal/external link counts, social performance, outdated-information presence, thin-content indicators, cannibalization with other content.

### Step 3: Decide Content Fate
Flag content with: zero organic/general traffic, minimal internal/external links, poor social performance, outdated information, thin content, cannibalization issues.

## Decision Alternatives (not just deletion)
- **Improve**: refresh title tags/meta descriptions/headings, add recent developments, remove outdated sections.
- **Trim, update, move**: consolidate related pieces, merge weak pages into a single stronger article, add update dates/disclaimers for aging content.
- **Make non-indexable**: noindex low-SEO-value-but-user-useful pages (e.g. blog tag pages); canonicalize duplicate PDFs to the main page.
- **Remove with caution**: delete only after staged rollout; monitor impact 1-3 weeks between deletion batches; use "severity buckets" based on traffic/backlinks/engagement.

## Tools
Conductor, Google Search Console, Bing Webmaster Tools, Ahrefs (backlinks), Screaming Frog (crawling/word count), Google Analytics, BuzzSumo (social).
