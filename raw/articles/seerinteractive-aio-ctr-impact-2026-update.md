# AIO Impact on Google CTR: 2026 Update (Seer Interactive)

Source: https://www.seerinteractive.com/insights/aio-impact-on-google-ctr-2026-update
Authors: Tracy McDonald, Hannah Cooley, Marketa Williams
Published: 2026-04-24
Fetched: 2026-07-07
Note: third installment, updating the original February 2025 study and a
September 2025 update.

## Methodology

53 brands, 5.47 million tracked queries, 2.43 billion organic
impressions, January 2025 through February 2026, with March 2026
projections. Expanded from the original 42-brand, informational-only
approach to include informational, transactional, and commercial intent.
Intent classified via SERP features; AIO status labels are static
snapshots applied retrospectively (not real-time). Forecasts use OLS
linear regression on 12 months of 2025 data per segment, no seasonality
adjustment.

AIO status classifications used throughout:
- **No AIO**: no AI Overview shown
- **AIO – Brand Not Cited**: AI Overview shown, brand's domain not linked in it
- **AIO – Brand Cited**: AI Overview shown, brand's domain cited/linked as a source

## Seven key findings

1. **2025's decline did not extend into 2026.** "Every single
   informational and transactional organic segment beat our model's
   projection in both January and February 2026." Forecasted continuous
   decline; actual data shifted within the first two months of the year.

2. **The October–November 2025 CTR "drop" was a measurement artifact.**
   Brand-cited organic CTR fell 52.1% in October and 36.8% in November,
   but clicks stayed essentially flat (398,798 → 400,271 → 301,783) while
   impressions more than doubled (15.8M → 33.1M → 39.5M Sept-Nov). More
   queries earned citations; existing citations didn't stop converting.
   "A drop in AIO - brand Cited CTR is not automatically bad news."

3. **Non-AIO queries improved too.** Organic CTR on queries without AIOs
   rose from 2.8% (Jan 2025) to 3.2% (Dec 2025) to 3.8% (Feb 2026).
   Informational non-AIO queries (901M impressions) rose from 2.9% to 4%.

4. **Transactional queries recovered dramatically.** Brand-cited
   transactional CTR doubled from 0.7% (Jan 2025) to 1.7% (Dec 2025),
   after bottoming at 0.62% in September.

5. **Paid search is far more predictable than organic.** The model
   missed Paid CTR by only 0.09 percentage points in Jan 2026, but missed
   organic CTR by 0.6–2.6 points across every segment. "Paid is
   predictable because it's structurally depressed and stable. Organic
   is unpredictable because it's still being shaped by the AIO
   landscape... signals we can't fully model yet."

6. **Query format strongly predicts AIO prevalence** (30,842 tracked
   informational queries): comparison queries trigger AIOs 95.4% of the
   time (267/280), question-format 85.9% (1,214/1,413).

7. **Citation premium, quantified.** "Being cited in the AIO delivers
   +120% more organic clicks per impression versus when you are not
   cited. But it still underperforms No AIO by -38%." Per 1M
   informational impressions: No AIO ≈ 33,500 clicks; Brand Cited ≈
   20,743; AIO present/Brand Not Cited ≈ 9,445.

## AIO prevalence by intent and query format

- Informational queries: 36% show AIOs
- Commercial queries: 8%
- Transactional queries: 5% (7x gap vs. informational)

Query-format AIO trigger rates (49,353 distinct queries analyzed):
- Comparison ("X vs Y"): 95.4%
- Review queries: 86.3%
- Question-format: 85.9% (1.4M impressions)
- Price/cost/buy queries: 83.4%
- "Best of" queries: 81.3%
- "Near me" queries: 76.9% (surprising for local intent)
- Single-word queries: 27.3% (25M impressions — "don't assume short
  queries are safe")

## Channel patterns

**Organic**: being cited delivered 2–5x the CTR of not being cited,
consistently, across every month of 2025, even as absolute CTRs
compressed.

**Paid**: brand-cited paid CTR held steady between 13.99–17.95% all
year with no meaningful decline; non-AIO paid CTR stayed higher (20–26%)
but also stable. "Whatever AIO is doing to organic results, it is not
doing the same thing to paid ads."

## Strategic guidance

- Prioritize earning citations in AIOs for informational content
  (+120% clicks vs. not cited) — still valid.
- Track impressions and clicks separately, never read CTR alone.
- Commercial queries are *not* safe from AIO-adjacent CTR compression
  despite low direct AIO exposure — audit them separately.
- Comparison and question-format pages need AEO/GEO audits now, given
  95%/86% AIO trigger rates.
- Identify and double down on no-AIO query clusters — 901M impressions
  at improving CTR, representing differentiated-content opportunity.
- "A CTR drop isn't automatically a signal to pull back. Your brand may
  be showing up in the answer without earning the click. That's a
  different kind of win in the context of AIOs."

## Caveats (explicitly stated by the authors)

- Correlation, not causation — higher-authority brands are more likely
  to be cited, confounding the citation-premium numbers.
- Brand-cited impression changes can't be attributed to Google's
  algorithm vs. the brand's own SEO performance without account-level
  investigation.
- AIO status labels are static snapshots, not real-time.
- Linear forecasting models underestimate in recovery environments;
  March 2026 figures are directional floors, not predictions.
- The Informational/Brand-Not-Cited segment's 0.94% CTR is skewed by one
  account (47% of impressions at 0.20% CTR); excluding it raises the
  aggregate to ~1.61%.
- Commercial no-AIO organic CTR ranges 5.69–6.82% depending on how
  compound intents are grouped.

## Bottom line

"Measurement methodology matters. But the bigger insight is that brand
engagement may still be happening via the AIO citation itself... impressions
and clicks on your domain aren't the whole picture. Brand reach is
happening on off-property." The framing shift from 2025 to 2026: not
"AIOs are destroying organic traffic" but "understand where your brand
appears, whether you're cited, and which query types still drive value."
