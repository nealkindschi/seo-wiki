Chris Long, "SaaS SEO: The Ultimate Guide" (Go Fish Digital, 2023-09-10).

## Key takeaways

- Agrees with and extends [[semrush-saas-seo]]/[[saas-seo-strategy]]
  with a sharper, more contrarian framing: **don't expect single-vendor
  product pages to rank at all** for competitive category terms —
  aggregators (Capterra alone runs 750+ category pages) win those spots
  because users prefer comparing multiple vendors at once. This
  sharpens [[saas-seo-strategy]]'s existing "product pages ≠ where you
  win" implication into an explicit, quotable rule: "nobody wants a
  single vendor product page to rank" for the terms teams often waste
  the most budget chasing.
- **Aggregator-gap keyword research**: mine competitor Capterra
  profiles (category keywords, "Featured In" sections) and run a
  reverse-gap analysis (keywords competitors rank for but Capterra
  doesn't) — a concrete, SaaS-aggregator-specific keyword-research
  technique not in [[semrush-saas-seo]].
- **Page-type taxonomy**: product, features (one page per feature,
  long-tail), industries/vertical (worked example: "church text" won a
  featured snippet at 4,400 MSV), and alternatives pages (competitor
  comparisons) — a more granular page taxonomy than
  [[saas-seo-strategy]]'s ToFu/MoFu/BoFu content-type table; the
  "alternatives" page type is functionally the same tactic as that
  playbook's "[Product] vs. [Competitor]" comparison-keyword step,
  just named differently.
- **84.3-day average SaaS sales cycle** — a concrete number supporting
  why SaaS SEO must cover awareness→consideration→purchase, not just
  bottom-funnel product terms.
- **"Software" terminology tactic**: use the word "software" in
  targeting/copy even against brand-team resistance, when search
  volume data justifies it — a concrete cross-team-conflict resolution
  tactic (ties to [[saas-seo-strategy]]'s persona/problem-over-keyword
  framing, but resolves the opposite tension: sometimes the
  higher-volume generic term beats the brand-preferred one).
- **Tangential-content link building**: publish data studies/surveys on
  topics adjacent to (not directly about) your product to earn PR
  coverage and backlinks — case example: a "Remote Working Statistics"
  piece earned 95 backlinks from 69 domains. A distinct link-building
  angle from [[enterprise-seo-strategy]]'s publisher-partnership and
  unlinked-mention tactics — this one is about content topic selection
  specifically for linkability, not outreach mechanics.
- Case-study numbers (41% first-page ranking lift, HubSpot outranking
  MailChimp's own product page for "email marketing") are useful
  concrete examples but, like the Terakeet case stats already flagged
  in this wiki, are vendor/agency-reported rather than independently
  verified.
