# The Listicle Rank Effect (Peec AI)

**Citation:** Ehrlinspiel, Jan. "The Listicle Rank Effect: What Nearly
200,000 AI Responses Across 8 AI Engines Reveal About Brand
Visibility." Peec AI Blog. Published 2026-05-14.
https://peec.ai/blog/the-listicle-rank-effect-what-nearly-200-000-ai-responses-across-8-ai-engines-reveal-about-brand-visibility
(Methodology: ~200,000 AI responses, 5.7M+ data points, 8 engines —
ChatGPT, Claude Sonnet 4, Gemini, Google AI Mode, Google AI Overview,
GPT-5 Search, Microsoft Copilot, Perplexity — Sept 2025-Mar 2026, three
markets: B2B SaaS, Emerging MarTech, US Finance.)

## Key takeaways

- Establishes a **listicle rank effect**: rank position within a
  frequently-cited third-party listicle measurably shifts AI-response
  visibility, answer placement, and mention count — not just whether a
  brand appears in the listicle at all.
- Effect size and shape are **market-dependent**: sharpest/steepest in
  fragmented emerging markets (Emerging MarTech, +13.4pp at rank #1),
  large but less rank-ordered in trusted/small-pool markets (US
  Finance), flatter for established brands once already included (B2B
  SaaS, +16.5pp but low added rank-sensitivity).
- **Diminishing returns / concentration**: a handful of top placements
  in frequently-retrieved listicles outperforms many placements in
  rarely-retrieved ones ("five strong placements" beat "50 weak
  ones").
- **Engine retrieval strategy matters**: tight-retrieval engines
  (ChatGPT, GPT-5 Search, Copilot) show larger per-listicle lifts than
  broad-retrieval engines (Google AI Overview, AI Mode).
