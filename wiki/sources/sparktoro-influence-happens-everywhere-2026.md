# New Research: Influence Happens Everywhere (SparkToro)

**Citation:** Fishkin, Rand. "New Research: Influence Happens Everywhere,
an analysis of the 5,000 most-visited sites on the mobile and desktop
web." SparkToro Blog. Published 2026-03-25.
https://sparktoro.com/blog/new-research-influence-happens-everywhere-an-analysis-of-the-5000-most-visited-sites-on-the-mobile-and-desktop-web/
(Clickstream analysis via Similarweb partnership, top 5,000 sites,
January 2026 data; top 200 sites hand-categorized into 15 categories,
remaining 4,800 GPT-classified with hand-reviewed samples, ~99% claimed
accuracy.)

## Key takeaways

- **Search + social ≈ half of all web visits** across the top 5,000
  sites — the two largest channels by attention share.
- **Google dominates search, and search dominates overall traffic**:
  Google is ~73% of search activity, and gets more traffic than the next
  13 largest sites *combined*.
- **AI tools are a rounding error relative to their press coverage**: AI
  tool visits measured "1/1,000th the size of the breathless press
  coverage" they receive — a direct scale check against the assumption
  that AI answer engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, etc.) already
  represent major traffic volume relative to search/social.
- **Influence precedes search**: content consumed across social, news,
  email, and entertainment builds the awareness/interest that search
  later "converts" into an answer — search is not where demand
  originates.
- **Attribution bias**: marketers over-credit search (a concentrated,
  easy-to-measure channel) and under-credit fragmented channels (social,
  news) that actually generate the underlying awareness.
- **The real AI competitive threat is Google's own AI integration**
  (AI Overviews/AI Mode embedded in search), not standalone AI chat
  tools — because of the traffic-scale gap above, Google-embedded AI is
  where visibility competition actually matters most right now.

## Noted limitations (per the source)

- Mobile app data excluded — social, email, and messaging usage would
  likely read larger if included.
- AI tool usage may be inflated by desktop skew in the underlying
  Similarweb data.
- Gmail traffic redirects to Google.com, undercounting email as a
  distinct channel.
- Includes some non-influential malware-driven visits.

## Relationship to existing wiki claims

- **No conflict** with [[ai-citation-landscape]] or [[aio-ctr-impact]] —
  those measure citation composition and click economics *given* someone
  is already using a search/AI surface. This source measures the
  *relative size* of AI tools as a traffic channel in the first place, a
  dimension not previously covered anywhere in the wiki.
- **Adds a load-bearing scale caveat** to
  [[generative-engine-optimization]] and the wiki's broader AI-search
  framing: essentially every other source in this wiki studies AI
  citation/visibility mechanics on the *assumption* that AI answer
  surfaces are a major, growing traffic channel worth optimizing for.
  This source doesn't contradict that AI surfaces are growing, but adds
  a currently-missing scale check: as of the source's Jan 2026 data,
  standalone AI tools remain tiny relative to search/social, and the
  most consequential AI-visibility work is arguably Google-embedded
  AI (AI Overviews/AI Mode) rather than third-party AI chat tools.
