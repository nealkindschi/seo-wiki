# Google's Guide to Optimizing for Generative AI Features on Google Search

**Citation:** Google Search Central. "Google's Guide to Optimizing for
Generative AI Features on Google Search." Last updated 2026-06-29 UTC.
https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/ai-optimization-guide

## Key takeaways

- Official first-party position: **"SEO is still relevant"** — Google's
  AI features (AI Overviews, AI Mode) are built on the same core Search
  ranking/quality systems, using retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) and
  "query fan-out" over the existing index, rather than a separate ranking
  system.
- Explicitly says these do **not** help: `llms.txt` / special AI text
  files or markup, "chunking" content into tiny pieces, obsessing over
  structured data, rewriting content specifically "for AI," or pursuing
  inauthentic mentions/backlinks.
- Structured data is "not required" for generative AI search but still
  recommended as part of general SEO.
- Core recommendation is unique, "helpful, reliable, and people-first"
  content with genuine expertise, clear technical crawlability, and (for
  commerce/local) Merchant Center feeds and Business Profiles.
