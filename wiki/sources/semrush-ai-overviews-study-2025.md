# Semrush AI Overviews Study: What 2025 SEO Data Tells Us About Google's Search Shift

**Citation:** Garanko, Jana (Semrush), with Luke Harsel, Anna Yudina,
Aleksandr Drozdov, Christine Skopec. "Semrush AI Overviews Study: What
2025 SEO Data Tells Us About Google's Search Shift." Semrush Blog.
Originally published March 2025, updated December 15, 2025.
https://www.semrush.com/blog/semrush-ai-overviews-study/
(10M+ keywords for prevalence tracking; 10K keywords for SERP
co-occurrence; 200K+ keywords for zero-click trends; 11K domains for
industry impact; Datos clickstream partnership.)

## Key takeaways

- **AIO prevalence rose sharply then pulled back in 2025**: 6.49% of
  queries (start of year) → peaked 24.61% (July) → settled at 15.69%
  (November) — a 155% Q1-to-Q4 growth with a mid-year peak-then-retreat
  shape.
- **AIOs expanded well beyond informational queries**: commercial
  8.15%→18.57%, transactional 1.98%→13.94%, navigational 0.84%→10.33%
  over 2025.
- **Zero-click rate actually decreased** (33.75%→31.53%) on keywords
  tracked before/after AIO introduction — AIOs did not automatically
  suppress clicks in this dataset.
- **Industry saturation**: Science most AIO-saturated (25.96%), followed
  by Computers & Electronics (17.92%) and People & Society (17.29%);
  Food & Drink grew fastest (+7.25% since March); Real Estate, Shopping,
  and Arts & Entertainment least impacted (<3%).
- **AIO-triggering keywords skew long-tail**: ~60% have ≤100 monthly
  searches, 60% fall in the 21-60 keyword-difficulty range.
- **SERP feature co-occurrence shifted**: featured snippets, image
  carousels, and organic reviews co-occur less with AIOs; Related
  Searches (95.32%) and People Also Ask (90.03%) are now nearly
  guaranteed alongside an AIO; Google Ads co-occurrence jumped from <1%
  (March) to 25% (November).
- **Framing**: "SEO becomes more about presence than position" as AIOs
  push Google toward an answer-engine model.

## Relationship to existing wiki claims

- **Extends** [[aio-ctr-impact]] (based on Seer Interactive's
  53-brand/5.47M-query study) with prevalence and zero-click data at a
  much larger keyword scale (10M+ vs. Seer's query-level tracking), but
  the two studies measure different things and use different time
  windows — flagged as complementary, not directly reconciled:
  - This source's **intent-based prevalence numbers** (informational not
    separately reported here beyond the intent breakdown above; commercial
    8.15-18.57%, transactional 1.98-13.94%, navigational 0.84-10.33%)
    are a *different snapshot* than [[aio-ctr-impact]]'s existing figures
    (informational 36%, commercial 8%, transactional 5%). Both can be
    correct as measurements of different points in time / different
    methodologies — not treated as a contradiction, just non-comparable
    without more detail on each study's exact sampling window.
  - This source's **zero-click rate decrease** (33.75%→31.53%) is a
    different metric than Seer's **organic CTR** data (which shows AIO
    presence still suppresses organic clicks 38% below a no-AIO
    baseline, even for cited brands). "Zero-click rate falling" and
    "organic CTR still below no-AIO baseline" are not mutually
    exclusive: more queries could be generating *some* click (fewer
    zero-click sessions) while the *rate* of clicks-per-impression on
    AIO-present queries remains suppressed relative to non-AIO queries.
    Both figures should be read as distinct, non-contradictory angles
    on the same broader phenomenon.
- **Net-new to the wiki**: industry-level AIO saturation data, AIO
  keyword-characteristic profile (length/volume/difficulty), and the
  SERP-feature co-occurrence shifts (Google Ads co-occurrence jump in
  particular is a notable, previously unrecorded data point).
