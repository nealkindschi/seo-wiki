# Anchor Text: A Data-Driven Guide (Ahrefs)

**Citation:** Hardwick, Joshua (reviewed by Sam Oh, Si Quan Ong).
"Anchor Text: A Data-Driven Guide." Ahrefs Blog. Published/updated
2020-04-03. https://ahrefs.com/blog/anchor-text/
(Methodology: Study 1 — 384,614 pages, 19,840 keywords, Spearman
correlation of anchor-text type vs. ranking. Study 2 — 16,000 pages
with random anchors, correlation of surrounding link-text relevance
vs. ranking.)

## Key takeaways

- Covers **backlink (external) anchor text** specifically — distinct
  scope from the wiki's existing internal-linking anchor-text
  guidance.
- **Every anchor-text type shows weak-to-negligible ranking
  correlation**: exact-match 0.14-0.19, phrase/partial-match ~0.11,
  random ~0.02 (Spearman). Surrounding link-text relevance correlates
  even less (0.06 down to slightly negative -0.07).
- **Core recommendation: don't manipulate anchor text ratios at all.**
  Let backlink anchor text occur naturally; the one deliberate
  exception is guest blogging, where a branded/generic anchor should
  be chosen over a keyword-rich one specifically to avoid looking
  manipulated.
- **Historical context**: anchor text was heavily weighted in Google's
  original 1998 PageRank algorithm, which created a manipulation
  incentive; the 2012 Penguin update specifically targeted
  overly-aggressive exact-match anchor link building (affecting ~3.1%
  of queries initially).
- **Only ~22% of a page's traffic comes from its single main target
  keyword** on average — most comes from long-tail variations, which
  undermines the premise of optimizing anchor text for one exact
  keyword.
- Acknowledged study limitation: no reliable way to define a single
  "primary keyword" per page at this scale, introducing correlation
  noise.
