# Why Proprietary Data Is Your Most Defensible AI Citation Asset (Growth Memo)

**Citation:** Indig, Kevin and Johnson, Amanda. "Why proprietary data is
your most defensible AI citation asset." Growth Memo. Published
2026-06-29. https://www.growth-memo.com/p/why-proprietary-data-is-your-most
(Underlying dataset: On-Page.ai information-gain research.)

## Key takeaways

- **Original data is the strongest predictor of page originality.**
  On-Page.ai found pages with 15+ unique figures averaged an information
  gain score of 62.1, vs. 40.2 for pages with minimal original data.
  Most pages are "middling on originality" — genuine data differentiation,
  not just effort, is what separates the two tiers.
- **Owning proprietary data doesn't guarantee the citation.** Aggregators
  can repackage someone else's original research into a cleaner format
  and capture the citation instead of the data's originator — ownership
  is necessary but not sufficient.
- **44.2% of all citations come from the first 30% of a page** — a
  concrete placement statistic quantifying how strongly early positioning
  drives citation capture.
- **Structural requirements for machine extraction**: lead with headline
  statistics in the first 30% of the page; define metrics immediately
  with context (don't assume the reader/model already knows what the
  number means); box methodology information separately from narrative;
  front-load secondary findings ordered by strength; cut suspenseful/
  narrative-buildup structures that delay the payoff.
- **Most businesses already have unused proprietary data** — product
  usage, pricing, customer behavior, performance metrics — that can be
  published as citation-ready content without a dedicated research
  budget.
