# AIO Impact on Google CTR: 2026 Update (Seer Interactive)

**Citation:** Tracy McDonald, Hannah Cooley, Marketa Williams. "AIO
Impact on Google CTR: 2026 Update." Seer Interactive, 2026-04-24. Third
installment, updating a February 2025 original and September 2025
update.
https://www.seerinteractive.com/insights/aio-impact-on-google-ctr-2026-update

## Key takeaways

- Largest-scale CTR study in the wiki so far: 53 brands, 5.47M tracked
  queries, 2.43B organic impressions, Jan 2025–Feb 2026.
- **Citation premium, quantified**: being cited in an AI Overview
  delivers **+120% more organic clicks per impression** than not being
  cited — but even cited traffic runs **-38% below** a no-AIO baseline.
  Per 1M informational impressions: No AIO ≈ 33,500 clicks; Cited ≈
  20,743; Not Cited ≈ 9,445.
- **Unexpected 2026 recovery**: after 18 months of decline through 2025,
  cited-brand organic CTR climbed from 1.3% (Dec 2025) to 2.4% (Feb
  2026), beating every forecast.
- **Measurement lesson**: a scary -52% single-month CTR drop (Oct 2025)
  was a measurement artifact — clicks held flat while impressions
  doubled (more citations earned, not lost conversions). Always separate
  impressions from clicks before reacting to a CTR change.
- **AIO prevalence is highly intent- and format-dependent**:
  informational queries show AIOs 36% of the time vs. 5% for
  transactional. Comparison queries trigger AIOs 95.4% of the time,
  question-format 85.9%, "near me" 76.9%, even single-word queries 27.3%.
- **Paid search is stable and predictable** (model missed by 0.09pp);
  **organic is volatile** (model missed by 0.6–2.6pp) — AIO affects the
  two channels very differently.
- Explicit caveats: correlation not causation, static (non-real-time)
  AIO labels, one account skewing the "not cited" segment, March 2026
  figures are projections not measurements.
