# Topics Matter for Third-Party Authority Signals (Growth Memo)

**Citation:** Indig, Kevin and Johnson, Amanda. "Topics Matter for
Third-Party Authority Signals." Growth Memo. Published 2026-06-15.
https://www.growth-memo.com/p/topics-matter-for-third-party-authority

## Key takeaways

- **AI source sets are topic-specific, not generic.** Which domains an
  AI engine treats as trustworthy shifts sharply by query topic — e.g.
  competitor domains make up 33.5% of citations for invoicing queries
  but only 7% for business-startup queries. Authority-building can't be
  a single generic campaign; it has to be mapped per topic/category.
- **Authority Score is the strongest single correlate of AI mentions**
  found in this analysis: 0.65 Pearson correlation across a 1,000-domain
  sample.
- **Authority gains are tiered, not linear.** Three placements in a
  top-decile-authority source outperform a dozen placements across
  low-authority sites — concentration in fewer, higher-authority
  outlets beats spreading effort thin.
- **Named, expert-authored content outperforms brand-account content.**
  Per LinkedIn's analysis (cited in the source), "fresh, expert-authored,
  clearly time-stamped content earned the fastest visibility" —
  individual bylines with credibility outperform faceless brand
  publishing accounts.
- **Recommended action steps**: identify 2-3 credible subject-matter
  experts; map which sources AI already cites per topic; concentrate PR/
  content efforts on high-authority publications in that map rather than
  broad low-authority placement; pursue nofollow opportunities (citation
  value doesn't require dofollow links); create embeddable data assets;
  use LinkedIn for rapid indexing/visibility.

## Relationship to existing wiki claims

- **Extends** [[ai-visibility-correlation-factors]]: that page already
  ranks Domain Rating (0.266–0.326) as a comparatively weak correlate of
  AI-mention visibility (Ahrefs' 75k-brand study). This source's 0.65
  Pearson correlation for **Authority Score** looks much stronger at
  first glance, but the two studies measure different metrics
  (Domain Rating vs. Authority Score) on different samples (75,000
  brands vs. 1,000 domains) — flagged below as a metric-comparability
  gap, not a direct contradiction, since Authority Score and Domain
  Rating are different Semrush/Ahrefs metrics with different
  construction.
- **Extends** [[ai-citation-landscape]]'s "category-relative authority"
  finding (per
  [[brightedge-ai-search-same-brands-different-sources]], which already
  argues authority should be treated as category-relative, not a fixed
  domain list) — this source adds a concrete number (33.5% vs. 7%
  competitor-domain share by topic) quantifying just how much source
  trust varies by topic, and extends it from "authority mix varies by
  engine" to "authority mix varies by topic within the same engine."
- **Extends** [[geo-content-optimization-tactics]]'s three-layer source
  strategy with two new tactical additions: (1) authority accumulation
  is tiered/concentrated rather than linear, and (2) named-author bylines
  outperform brand-account publishing — neither was previously in the
  wiki.
- No conflict with [[growth-memo-why-most-original-data-never-gets-cited]]
