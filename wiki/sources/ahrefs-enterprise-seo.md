Patrick Stox, "Enterprise SEO Strategies For Maximum Growth" (Ahrefs blog, updated 2025-01-21).

## Key takeaways

- **New territory for this wiki**: everything here about scale/
  organizational SEO — stakeholder buy-in, resource prioritization,
  reporting, org structure, evangelism — is a distinct layer this wiki
  didn't cover before, sitting *above* the tactical playbooks
  (content, links, technical) which mostly already exist here.
- Where content/technical/link tactics overlap existing pages, they
  agree and extend rather than conflict:
  - Consolidating cannibalized content into one comprehensive guide +
    redirecting weaker pages = the same fix
    [[keyword-mapping-and-cannibalization]] and
    [[content-pruning-playbook]] already recommend, now with an
    enterprise-scale framing (do this systematically across a large
    site, not case-by-case).
  - Redirecting 404s with existing backlinks, and the "~2/3 of links
    disappear over 9 years" stat, reinforces
    [[content-pruning-playbook]]'s redirect-discipline point with a
    concrete decay rate.
  - Recovering unlinked brand mentions (via Content Explorer) is the
    same category of tactic as [[ahrefs-image-seo]]'s image-backlink
    link-equity recovery — "find where you're already mentioned/linked
    imperfectly and fix it" as a repeatable link-building motion.
  - High/medium/specialized technical-priority tiering matches
    [[technical-seo-audit-checklist]]'s existing content, adding an
    enterprise-specific "specialized" tier (hreflang, crawl budget at
    millions-of-pages scale, ecommerce faceted nav, M&A traffic
    integration) not previously broken out.
- **New, not previously covered**: stakeholder buy-in messaging
  (concrete stats to use: 90.63% of pages get zero organic traffic;
  average top page ranks for ~1,000 other keywords), the Impact-Effort
  Matrix for prioritization, tiered reporting by audience level, org
  structure/evangelism tactics (lunch-and-learns, internal newsletters,
  opportunistic team involvement), a card-sorting content-brief
  process, and an automatable redirect-matching criteria set
  (>3 referring domains, >5 hits/month).
- **Enterprise-scale benchmarks**: Amazon.com (275M ranking pages,
  686M monthly organic visits), Microsoft.com (516M monthly organic
  visits) — useful reference points for "what does enterprise scale
  actually look like."
- **Local SEO enterprise stats**: 78% of nearby-phone-searchers visit
  within a day, 28% of nearby searches convert to purchase, 30% of
  mobile searches are location-related.
