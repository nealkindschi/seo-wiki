Jonas Sickler, "Enterprise SEO: A Marketer's Guide to Search Optimization" (Terakeet, 2020-08-03).

## Key takeaways

- Agrees with and extends [[ahrefs-enterprise-seo]]/[[enterprise-seo-strategy]]
  with an older (2020) but distinct emphasis: **market-share growth and
  brand-reputation control** as the defining goal of enterprise SEO,
  vs. Ahrefs' more tactical/process framing. Older source, no
  conflicts — different angle on the same discipline.
- **Brand reputation protection via SERP control**: shore up owned
  properties and preferred third-party content across the search
  landscape so a crisis (negative press, a lawsuit, bad reviews) has
  less room to dominate branded-search results — a genuinely new
  tactic category not in [[enterprise-seo-strategy]] or
  [[brand-entity-seo-strategy]]. Case study cited: a financial brand
  losing leads to negative articles recovered SERP market share after
  12 months of optimization/link building. Ties to
  [[geo-content-optimization-tactics]]'s sentiment-monitoring section,
  but at the classic-SERP level rather than AI-answer level.
- **4-tier keyword funnel framework** — Research (awareness,
  non-branded) → Category (consideration, e.g. "women's boots") →
  Product (consideration/decision) → Branded (decision, narrative
  control) — a funnel-stage lens to layer onto
  [[keyword-mapping-and-cannibalization]]'s page-to-keyword mapping
  process; that playbook maps keywords to pages, this framework adds
  *which funnel stage* each mapped keyword serves.
- **Content atomization**: repurpose one core content asset across
  formats (blog, video, infographic, webinar, ebook, podcast), and
  customize roughly 75% of "standard" content per industry segment
  when serving multiple verticals — a production-efficiency tactic not
  previously in the wiki.
- **Duplicate-content consolidation stat**: audits sometimes warrant
  consolidating or pruning 50%+ of bloated content — a concrete
  magnitude to set expectations with, extending
  [[content-pruning-playbook]].
- **Automation/tooling infrastructure list**: BPM/workflow software
  (Process Street, Pipefy, Kissflow) and reporting dashboards (Domo,
  Tableau, DashThis, Grow) — fills in the specific tooling category
  [[enterprise-seo-strategy]]'s reporting/process sections didn't name
  concretely.
- **Publisher and corporate partnership tactics**: pitching content
  angles tailored to a specific outlet's focus (innovation for CNBC,
  sustainability for NPR) and co-branded/cross-promotional corporate
  partnerships — extends [[enterprise-seo-strategy]]'s link-building
  section with named partnership types beyond unlinked-mention
  recovery and redirect reclamation.
- **Cross-department integration benefits, spelled out per department**
  (paid search, PR, social, UX/dev) — more specific than
  [[enterprise-seo-strategy]]'s general org-collaboration guidance.
- Impressive but vendor-reported case-stats (265% market-share growth,
  429% value-per-visit, 11.3x pageviews, $32M recovered revenue) — note
  these are the source's own client results, not independently
  verified third-party research; treat as directional/marketing
  evidence rather than a benchmark to promise clients.
