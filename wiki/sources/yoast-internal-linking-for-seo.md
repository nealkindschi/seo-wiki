# Internal linking for SEO: why and how (Yoast)

**Citation:** Qureshi, Ahad. "Internal linking for SEO: why and how."
Yoast. Published 2025-11-12.
https://yoast.com/internal-linking-for-seo-why-and-how/

## Key takeaways

- Reinforces link taxonomy (navigational/contextual/footer/sidebar) and
  the pillar-cluster content-hub model already in the wiki, with a
  concrete worked example (pillar "coffee machine" + long-tail clusters
  linking both directions).
- **Cornerstone content pattern**: designate your most comprehensive
  article on a topic as "cornerstone" content; link shorter/supporting
  posts *to* it, and link back *from* it to the supporting posts —
  a two-way linking pattern within a cluster.
- **Taxonomies as structure**: category/tag pages (not just pillar
  pages) can serve as internal-linking hubs that signal site structure
  to Google — e.g. linking into a "Technical SEO" tag page.
- **Related/popular posts modules**: sidebar/footer widgets and
  end-of-article "related posts" sections distribute link equity to
  top performers and reduce bounce rate.
- **New-content linking discipline**: after publishing, link to the new
  page from topically similar existing pages to avoid orphaning — but
  only from genuinely relevant pages, not indiscriminately.
- **Anchor text nuance**: recommends "exact phrases when possible" (e.g.
  "Best coffee machines") mixed with natural variations. This leans
  slightly more keyword-forward than Google's own guidance.
- GEO framing: internal links act as context signals for AI/generative
  search systems, not just classic Google Search.
- No numeric link-count guidance given (unlike Ahrefs' "3-5") —
  consistent with the wiki's current Google-aligned "no cap" position.
- Tool mention: Yoast SEO Premium's smart-linking suggestions,
  breadcrumbs/related-links/subpages blocks, and link-distribution
  insights (WordPress-specific, not evaluated further here).

## Nuance vs. existing guidance

- **Claim**: anchor text should use exact-match keyword phrases where
  possible.
  - Supported by: this source — "use exact phrases when possible."
  - In tension with: [[google-links-crawlable]] — "resist the urge to
    cram every keyword," write "as naturally as possible," and
    [[link-and-anchor-text-best-practices]]'s existing "avoid keyword
    stuffing" guidance.
  - **Current best guess**: per the user's standing preference to trust
    Google's guidance over third-party SEO blogs when they diverge
    (established during the Ahrefs link-count ingest), keep the
    playbook's anchor-text guidance as-is (favor natural, varied,
    descriptive phrasing) rather than adding an "exact-match when
    possible" rule. Noted here for reference, not added as a
    contradicting instruction in the playbook itself.
