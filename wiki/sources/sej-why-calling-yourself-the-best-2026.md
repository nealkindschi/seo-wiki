# Why Calling Yourself The Best Could Be Helping Your Competitors Win In AI Search (SEJ)

**Citation:** Ray, Lily (Founder, Algorythmic). "Why Calling Yourself
The Best Could Be Helping Your Competitors Win In AI Search." Search
Engine Journal. Published 2026-06-18.
https://www.searchenginejournal.com/why-calling-yourself-the-best-could-be-helping-your-competitors-win-in-ai-search/579778/
(323 tracked citation instances; 100 B2B queries analyzed; named
examples: Oasis LMS, Kredo Learning, Pylon, TMetric, Pollfish.)

## Key takeaways

- **Citation and recommendation are decoupling in Google AI
  Overviews**: a self-promotional "best of" listicle can be cited as a
  source while the AI's actual recommendation goes to a *competitor*
  named within that same listicle — a 69% failure rate (224/323
  tracked instances) and present in ~74% of the 100 B2B queries
  analyzed.
- **Authority moderates the effect**: already-established, high-DR
  brands are sometimes exempted; the citation-without-recommendation
  penalty falls disproportionately on lesser-known companies even when
  they rank organically.
- **Reported Google countermeasures**: organic ranking demotion for
  domains with excessive self-promotional content, new AI Overview
  disclaimer language flagging "self-proclaimed expert" sources, and a
  citation-source shift toward third-party review platforms (Reddit,
  Forbes Advisor, YouTube) for "best of" queries.
- **Trend and timing**: self-promotional listicles surged through
  2025 (184 listicles, 146 brands); affected sites saw organic-
  visibility declines starting January 2026, accelerating through May
  2026 — plausibly the algorithmic correction described above kicking
  in after the tactic became widespread.
- Cites Pew Research's ~1% click-through rate on AI-summary citations
  to reinforce that citation alone has limited direct value —
  recommendation, not citation, is the metric that actually matters
  for AI-driven business outcomes.

## Conflict handling

**Apparent tension, not logged as a formal conflict**: this source
reports Google actively countermeasuring self-promotional listicles
(organic demotion, disclaimers) with declines visible from January
2026, overlapping the same window (Dec 2025-Feb 2026) where
[[peec-ai-self-promotional-listicles-2026]] found "no algorithmic
correction" in raw citation *rate*. Reconciled as measuring different
mechanisms rather than contradicting: Peec measured whether
self-promotional listicles keep getting *cited* at all (rate stayed
~11%, stable); this source measures whether being cited still leads to
an actual *recommendation* (a newer, narrower failure mode layered on
top of stable citation rates) and organic-traffic-level countermeasures
that a citation-rate metric alone wouldn't capture. Both can be true
simultaneously — citation rate held steady while the citation-to-
recommendation conversion and organic visibility specifically
degraded. Noted inline rather than as an unresolved Conflicting
Evidence block, since the two studies aren't measuring the same
variable.
