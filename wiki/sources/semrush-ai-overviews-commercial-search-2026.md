# AI Overviews Are Expanding Across Commercial Intent Search (Semrush)

**Citation:** Harsel, Luke (Semrush), with Anna Yudina and Christine
Skopec. "AI Overviews are expanding across commercial intent search
[Study]." Semrush Blog. Published 2026-07-02.
https://www.semrush.com/blog/ai-overviews-commercial-search-study/
(600,000+ keywords, US desktop database, November 2025-April 2026;
same research team as [[semrush-ai-overviews-study-2025]].)

## Key takeaways

- **Commercial-intent AIOs grew 71%** over the study period (Nov
  2025-Apr 2026); **transactional-intent AIOs decreased 5%** over the
  same window — a notable divergence from the earlier growth phase.
- Finance led commercial-intent AIO growth (+231%), followed by
  Computers & Electronics (+107.62%) and Games (+76.66%).
- **Google Ads + AI Overview coexistence on the same SERP is now ~2x
  more frequent** than a year prior — AIOs have moved well beyond
  low-intent informational queries into ad-adjacent commercial territory.
- **AIOs cluster on the highest-CPC keywords** in nearly every industry:
  Jobs & Education sees AIOs on >60% of its most expensive keywords;
  average CPC with-AIO vs. without: Jobs & Education $5.02 vs. $1.51,
  Finance $4.84 vs. $2.14.
- Largest commercial-vs-transactional AIO gaps: Internet & Telecom,
  Finance, Travel (longer research-phase categories). Food & Drink is
  inverted (16% transactional vs. 14% commercial) — quick-decision
  categories show less AIO integration.
- **Strategic framing**: marketers now compete across four SERP
  positions simultaneously — within the AI Overview summary itself,
  among its citation sources, in organic rankings, and in paid
  placements.

## Relationship to existing wiki claims

- **Extends** [[aio-ctr-impact]]'s existing Semrush-sourced data
  ([[semrush-ai-overviews-study-2025]]: commercial 8.15%→18.57%,
  transactional 1.98%→13.94% through calendar 2025; Google Ads
  co-occurrence <1%→25%) with a later time window (Nov 2025-Apr 2026)
  from the same research team.
- **Notable trend shift, not a conflict**: the earlier study showed
  transactional-intent AIO prevalence *rising* sharply through 2025;
  this source shows it *declining* 5% in the following months while
  commercial-intent AIOs kept climbing (+71%). Read together, this is
  consistent with the earlier study's own finding that overall AIO
  prevalence "peaked in July [2025] then settled lower by November" —
  the same peak-and-partial-retreat pattern may be playing out
  specifically within transactional intent on a lag, while commercial
  intent is still in its growth phase. Flagged as a real trend
  divergence worth monitoring, not a data contradiction (different
  time windows, same methodology/team).
- **Extends** the CPC/AIO relationship: the earlier study didn't report
  CPC-tier breakdowns; this source adds the finding that AIOs
  concentrate specifically on the *highest*-CPC keywords, which sharpens
  the earlier "Google Ads co-occurrence rising" finding — it's not just
  more ads near AIOs generally, but concentrated on the most valuable
  keywords.
- No conflicts.
