# The Downstream Impact of AI Visibility (Similarweb)

**Citation:** Similarweb. "The Downstream Impact of AI Visibility."
Published 2026-06-24.
https://www.similarweb.com/corp/the-downstream-impact-of-ai-visibility/
(Opted-in US desktop panel, July-December 2025, Finance/Travel/Beauty
verticals, named competitive brand pairs: American Express vs. Capital
One, Skyscanner vs. Kayak, Sephora vs. Ulta.)

## Key takeaways

- **First study in this wiki connecting AI mention/recommendation to
  measured downstream traffic**, rather than citation composition or
  click-through economics alone: brands recommended by ChatGPT are
  **2.5x more likely to receive a visit within 7 days** than a
  non-recommended direct competitor, consistent across all three
  verticals studied.
- **Industry-level visit-rate gaps**: American Express 7.2% vs. Capital
  One 3.1% (Finance); Skyscanner 9.5% vs. Kayak 7.6% (Travel); Sephora
  7.9% vs. Ulta 3.3% (Beauty).
- **Attribution-methodology finding — likely the most consequential
  result for measurement practice**: 55.9% of AI-influenced traffic
  arrives via search, not as a direct ChatGPT referral, and only 19.9%
  arrives as direct traffic. A user gets an AI recommendation, then
  searches the brand name — standard analytics records this as ordinary
  branded organic search, invisibly absorbing AI's actual influence.
- **AI-influenced visitors engage substantially harder**: 12.0 pages /
  11.8 minutes per session vs. 6.5 pages / 5.6 minutes for
  non-AI-influenced visitors — roughly 2x on both dimensions.
- **Explicit caveats from Similarweb**: US desktop only (excludes
  mobile/international); three verticals/named brand pairs, not a
  whole-web sample; correlational, not proven causal.

## Relationship to existing wiki claims

- **Extends** [[ai-citation-landscape]]'s new citations-vs-mentions
  distinction (from
  [[similarweb-how-to-be-the-brand-ai-recommends-2026]]) with hard
  traffic evidence: this source demonstrates that *mentions* alone (a
  ChatGPT recommendation, independent of whether the user clicks a
  citation link) measurably move downstream visit rates — direct
  confirmation of that page's "mentions build presence" claim.
- **Significant methodological implication for** [[aio-ctr-impact]] and
  [[ai-traffic-scale-vs-hype]]: the 55.9%-arrives-via-search finding
  suggests AI's real traffic influence is likely **undercounted** by any
  referral-based measurement (including SparkToro's clickstream
  analysis in [[ai-traffic-scale-vs-hype]], which measures direct visits
  to AI tools and referrals from them, not downstream searches an AI
  session triggered). This doesn't contradict SparkToro's finding that
  AI tools are a small fraction of overall traffic *in absolute visit
  terms* — it adds a caveat that some of the traffic currently
  attributed to "search" in that framing may actually be AI-influenced,
  meaning AI's true causal footprint could be larger than raw
  AI-tool-visit counts suggest, even if AI tools themselves remain a
  small direct-traffic channel.
- **Reinforces (independent, differently-measured)** the "AI-referred
  visitor conversion premium" data point already in [[aio-ctr-impact]]
  (from [[semrush-optimize-for-agentic-web]]: "AI visitor worth 4.4x
  more in conversion value") — this source measures session depth/time
  rather than conversion value, but points the same direction: AI-
  influenced visitors are meaningfully higher-engagement than average.
- No conflicts — correlational caveats are explicitly stated by the
  source itself and preserved here rather than overstated.
