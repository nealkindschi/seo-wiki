# How to Be the Brand AI Recommends (Similarweb)

**Citation:** Mordecai, Darrell (Similarweb). "How to Be the Brand AI
Recommends: How to get understood, trusted, and mentioned." Similarweb
(PDF report, 18 pages). No publication date stated in the document;
downloaded/ingested 2026-07-07.

A companion vendor playbook to
[[similarweb-how-to-win-the-race-for-gen-ai-search-2026]] (same
publisher, overlapping "topical authority" and "off-page authority"
framing). Uses Similarweb's own tool for illustrative examples (HubSpot
topical-coverage screenshot, American Eagle recommendations-agent
screenshot) — demo data, not independent research findings.

## Key takeaways

- **Citations vs. brand mentions is a genuinely useful conceptual
  split**, sharper than anything currently in the wiki: a **mention** is
  the AI saying a brand's name (can come from training data OR live
  retrieval); a **citation** is the AI pointing to a brand's content as
  a retrieved source (citations only come from retrieval). "Citations
  drive traffic, mentions build presence" — and mentions matter as a
  metric in their own right because most AI interactions end without a
  click.
- **Two data sources framing**: training data (frozen snapshot, months-
  to-years update cycle, only covers brands present before the training
  cutoff, high confidence once present) vs. live retrieval (real-time,
  any crawlable brand can appear, thinner/lower-confidence profile).
  Established brands benefit from both; newer brands depend almost
  entirely on live retrieval, making a crawlable third-party presence
  essential.
- **Recommendation signals vs. citation signals are different metrics**:
  recommendation/mention signals include topical co-occurrence
  frequency, branded search volume, presence in Wikipedia/Wikidata/major
  publications, and consistent positive co-mentions; citation signals
  include unique data points (information gain), content freshness,
  clear extractable structure, and co-citation across multiple
  independent sources.
- **AI visits are growing much faster than AI referral traffic** — a
  Similarweb clickstream chart shows the gap between visits to AI tools
  (ChatGPT/Gemini/Claude) and referrals *from* AI to other sites widening
  over 2025-2026. Directionally consistent with, and corroborating,
  [[aio-ctr-impact]]'s zero-click framing and
  [[ai-traffic-scale-vs-hype]]'s "most AI interactions end without a
  click" theme — though this is a different, narrower metric (AI-tool
  visit growth vs. referral growth specifically, not overall AI-tool
  traffic share vs. search/social).
- **Aleyda Solis's measurement heuristic**: don't chase perfection on
  single prompts — measure AI visibility as **sustained topical
  authority**, targeting presence in roughly 70-80% of relevant,
  high-intent prompts within a topic cluster, not isolated query wins.
  A new, concrete visibility-measurement target not previously in the
  wiki.
- **Independent corroboration of the 44%-of-citations-from-first-30%
  stat**: this source states "44% of AI citations come from the first
  30% of a page," matching (to within rounding) the 44.2% figure already
  in the wiki from
  [[growth-memo-why-proprietary-data-is-your-most-defensible-asset]].
  Two independent analyses converging on the same number strengthens
  confidence in this specific figure.
- **Topical clustering mechanics**: AI breaks a user query into
  sub-queries and searches across all of them simultaneously — a site
  with well-developed topic/subtopic silos surfaces consistently across
  sub-queries, while a scattered site surfaces in only 1-2 sub-queries
  and builds a fuzzy AI entity profile.
