# 43 B2B SEO Statistics for 2025 (Ahrefs)

**Citation:** Ong, Si Quan (reviewed by Ryan Law). "43 B2B SEO
Statistics for 2025." Ahrefs Blog. Published 2025-11-12.
https://ahrefs.com/blog/b2b-seo-statistics/
(A compilation of third-party stats, each individually attributed to
its original source in the raw file.)

## Key takeaways used in this wiki

- **Google still dwarfs standalone AI tools in traffic**: 345x more
  traffic than ChatGPT/Gemini/Perplexity combined — directionally
  reinforces [[sparktoro-influence-happens-everywhere-2026]]'s
  "1/1,000th" finding in [[ai-traffic-scale-vs-hype]] with an
  independent, differently-sourced ratio.
- **AI Overview citation composition**: 76% of AIO citations pull from
  Google's own top-10 organic results (reinforcing
  [[airops-fan-out-effect-2026]]'s retrieval-rank-as-gatekeeper finding
  specifically within Google's own AIO surface), and 28.9% of AIO
  citations concentrate on just the top 50 brands in a category.
- **A meaningful visibility gap exists**: 26% of brands get zero AI
  Overview mentions at all, and — a genuine nuance worth flagging —
  28% of ChatGPT's most-cited pages have **zero Google organic
  visibility**, meaning citation and classic organic ranking are
  correlated but not equivalent; a page can earn real AI citation
  share without ranking organically at all.
- **Freshness preference, an independent data point**: AI platforms
  show a 25.7% stronger preference for fresh content than traditional
  search — an additional data point for the wiki's unresolved
  freshness Conflicting Evidence (see
  [[ahrefs-why-chatgpt-cites-pages-2026]]'s conflicting age findings).
- **AI-content adoption is now mainstream but risky at scale**: 87% of
  marketers use AI for content creation, at ~4.7x lower cost and 42%
  higher output volume than non-AI-using marketers — raises the
  existing Scaled Content Abuse caution in
  [[e-e-a-t-and-page-quality]] as a live, common risk rather than a
  hypothetical one.
- **B2B-specific economics**: organic search converts at 5.0% on
  average with ~702% ROI and a much lower cost-per-lead than paid
  search in B2B SaaS specifically — useful business-case framing for
  [[generative-engine-optimization]]/[[classic-seo-ranking-factors]]
  investment.
- **B2B buyer journey context** (not directly SEO-actionable but
  relevant background): buying groups average 10-11 stakeholders over
  an 11.5-month cycle, 81% already have a preferred vendor before
  first outreach, and 71% ultimately choose their first shortlisted
  option — reinforces why early-stage visibility (getting on the
  shortlist at all, well before a formal RFP) matters more than
  late-funnel conversion tactics in B2B specifically.
