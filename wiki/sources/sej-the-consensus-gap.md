# The Consensus Gap (Search Engine Journal)

**Citation:** Indig, Kevin. "The Consensus Gap." Search Engine Journal.
Published 2026-05-12.
https://www.searchenginejournal.com/the-consensus-gap/574534/
(Underlying dataset: Omnia, 3.7M URL citations, four samples Q3
2025–Q1 2026.)

## Key takeaways

- Direct, large-scale (3.7M citations) confirmation of extreme
  cross-engine citation fragmentation: only **2.35-2.45%** of cited
  URLs for the same prompt appear in ChatGPT, Perplexity, *and* Google
  AI Overviews together; **91%** appear in only one engine. Holds
  nearly flat across four samples spanning Q3 2025–Q1 2026 — not a
  one-off snapshot.
- Even the most authoritative domains have low cross-engine
  portability: Wikipedia (16,073 citations) is universal in only 1.3%
  of cases; Reddit (14,267 citations) in 0.1%; Reuters (1,202
  citations) in 0.0%.
- Content-type portability ranking (highest to lowest): guides/
  tutorials (2.3%) > blogs (1.8%) > category pages (1.6%) > product
  pages (1.2%) > homepages (1.1%) — explanatory content travels best,
  though all figures are low in absolute terms.
- Proposes three distinct visibility metrics that a blended "AEO score"
  hides: **presence** (appears in any engine), **portability** (appears
  in all engines), **concentration** (share from a single engine) — and
  argues teams should measure and act on these separately rather than
  one composite number.
- Names the phenomenon **"the consensus gap"**: engines draw from
  largely disjoint source pools rather than ranking a shared pool
  differently — a structural claim about *why* the fragmentation
  exists, not just that it exists.
- Practical reframe: ask "how do we build assets that work across
  engine preferences" rather than "how do we rank in AI" — since
  engines favor utility (does this content directly answer the query
  well) over brand centrality (is this a well-known brand).

## Relationship to existing wiki claims

- **Reinforces** [[ai-citation-landscape]]'s "Providers are three
  separate information environments" finding (originally from
  [[muckrack-generative-pulse-ai-reading-may-2026]], covering
  ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini) with an independent dataset covering a
  different engine set (ChatGPT/Perplexity/Google AI Overviews),
  larger scale (3.7M vs. 25M+ citation *links*, but this source is
  explicitly overlap-measured at the prompt level), and longitudinal
  stability (~2% flat across a full year). Same conclusion, different
  engines, different methodology — strong independent confirmation.
- **Bears on** the unresolved Conflicting Evidence in
  [[ai-citation-landscape]] and [[ai-visibility-correlation-factors]]
  (citation-level fragmentation per Muck Rack vs. brand-mention-level
  correlation per Ahrefs). This source adds further weight to the
  citation/URL-level fragmentation side specifically — it does not
  test brand-level mention overlap, so it doesn't directly resolve the
  brand-vs-URL reconciliation, but it does make "citation-level
  fragmentation is real and large" a much better-supported premise of
  that reconciliation (now backed by two independent large-scale
  studies on two different engine sets, not one).
