Kevin Indig, "3 Advanced SaaS SEO Strategies & Best Practices" (Search Engine Journal, 2020-10-01).

## Key takeaways

- Extends [[saas-seo-strategy]] with a **scalable content-format**
  angle not covered by that playbook's ToFu/MoFu/BoFu or page-type
  framing — these are structural content *systems* rather than
  individual pages:
  1. **Learn hubs**: a topic-organized knowledge center that
     systematically targets keywords across a whole subject area while
     integrating product mentions into the educational content itself.
     Worked examples with real traffic: Zapier's learn center (5,000
     top-10 rankings, ~50K monthly sessions), Canva's resource hub
     (250K+ sessions from 5,500 top-10 keywords).
  2. **Marketplace formats**: a scalable page *template* applied to
     many similar entities (apps, integrations, service providers) —
     each individual page is thin, but the template scales to
     thousands of long-tail rankings. Examples: Shopify's app
     marketplace (140K+ monthly sessions, 3,500+ top-10 rankings),
     Zapier's integration directory (65K+ sessions).
  3. **Tools & calculators**: linkable, usable assets that double as a
     product gateway — earn backlinks *because* they're useful on their
     own, not because of outreach. Examples: Gusto's tool library
     (2,000 backlinks, 90K monthly sessions), Splunk's data-maturity
     calculator alone (5,800 backlinks).
- **"Alternatives to your product" framing vs. direct competitor
  comparison**: a subtly different angle from
  [[gofishdigital-seo-for-saas]]'s "alternatives pages" and
  [[saas-seo-strategy]]'s "[Product] vs. [Competitor]" comparison-
  keyword tactic — this source frames it as addressing the *searcher's*
  "alternatives to X" intent broadly, rather than a head-to-head
  comparison table against one named competitor. Complementary content
  angle, not a conflicting one.
- **Prioritize user value before SEO** in these formats specifically —
  the traffic numbers above are framed as a byproduct of genuine
  usefulness (a real tool, a real knowledge center), not of keyword
  targeting alone.
- **Market-growth context**: SaaS company count grew ~5,000% from
  2011-2020 — useful scale stat for why "basic" SaaS SEO tactics stop
  being enough as the category matures and gets crowded.
- **Stagnation-after-early-traction pattern**: SaaS startups often lose
  organic momentum once initial traction fades and teams shift budget
  to paid channels — flagged as a symptom that can mask an underlying
  product-market-fit problem rather than a pure marketing-channel
  problem. An organizational/diagnostic note, not a technical tactic.
- Older source (2020) but the traffic/backlink numbers cited are still
  useful as illustrative scale examples of what these formats can
  achieve at maturity, not as current benchmarks.
