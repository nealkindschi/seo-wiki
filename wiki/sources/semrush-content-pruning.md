Sydney Go, "Content Pruning" (Semrush blog, published/updated 2024-05-09).

## Key takeaways

- Agrees with [[conductor-content-pruning]] on the core model, framed as
  three actions instead of four: refresh, consolidate, remove (Conductor
  additionally separates out "make non-indexable" as a distinct fourth
  option — no real conflict, just a coarser vs. finer split of the same
  space).
- **7-step process** close to Conductor's 3-step version but more
  granular: define scope → identify low performers (with a concrete
  threshold example: 1,000 monthly organic sessions minimum) → identify
  technical/intent/competitive issues → **review backlink data before
  any removal decision** (pages with substantial backlinks should be
  refreshed/consolidated, not deleted) → label the problem type →
  assign refresh/consolidate/remove → release in stages, measuring
  impact before continuing.
- **Explicit remove-action risk**: removing a page creates a 404,
  losing its organic traffic and breaking any internal links pointing
  to it — reinforces [[content-pruning-playbook]]'s "remove is the last
  resort, staged" guidance with the concrete failure mode.
- **Case study**: Semrush's own blog saw a "substantial increase in
  organic traffic" from multi-year content refreshing — a first-party
  example of "refresh" (the lightest-touch option) working at scale,
  though no specific numbers are given.
- **Consolidate mechanics**: 301-redirect the weaker duplicate to the
  highest-quality version, explicitly to transfer link equity — same
  redirect-discipline point Conductor's sources raised.
