Gianluca Fiorelli, "The Role of SEO in Making Branding Understood by Search Engines and AI" (I Love SEO blog, 2024-12-11).

## Key takeaways

- **Core framing**: neither Google nor AI systems inherently understand
  a brand — a brand only becomes machine-legible once translated into
  entity signals (name/logo/design as distinctive characteristics,
  entity attributes as values, external trust/authority signals, and
  branded-query associations with other entities). This is the *brand*
  as the entity being optimized, distinct from
  [[entity-based-seo-implementation]]'s focus on entities *within*
  content.
- **Organization schema depth**: 40+ available properties; prioritize
  `sameAs` (links to business profiles) and hierarchical markup
  (`parentOrganization`/`subOrganization`) for corporate structures, plus
  `about`/`mention` to clarify entity relationships. Extends
  [[entity-based-seo-implementation]]'s `sameAs` guidance (which focuses
  on Wikipedia/Wikidata links for content entities) to the brand-entity
  and corporate-hierarchy level specifically.
- **Knowledge Graph data-source map**: Google's own ecosystem (Business,
  Merchant Center, YouTube), social profiles, "trusted seed" sites
  (Amazon, IMDB, Amazon Music), and secondary sources (Crunchbase,
  Glassdoor) all feed brand Knowledge Graph entries — gives a concrete
  audit checklist of *where* to establish/correct brand presence.
- **Branded query analysis as a strategy**: mine Google Search Console
  branded-query data, competitor branded queries, and product-specific
  branded searches (e.g. "Nike Jordan 1") to find content/menu-structure
  opportunities — the Games Workshop example (Italian searches
  over-indexing on one product line, suggesting menu restructuring) is
  a concrete worked case.
- **"Winning zone" content framework**: content should sit at the
  intersection of buyer personas, audience/influencer personas, and
  business objectives — a positioning framework distinct from, and
  complementary to, this wiki's citation-tactics-focused
  [[geo-content-optimization-tactics]] (that page optimizes *how* to
  write for citability; this framework decides *what* to write for
  brand-building).
- **Amplification/repurposing as its own workstream**: coordinated
  cross-channel sharing (owned, paid, influencer) creates measurable
  branded-search spikes (Spotify Wrapped's December spike, Games
  Workshop's YouTube-launch spike) — a distinct tactic from anything
  currently in the wiki's GEO/AEO playbooks, which focus on content
  properties rather than distribution/amplification.
- **Navboost / brand-bias data point**: Google's Navboost algorithm uses
  SERP engagement signals (CTR) for initial result filtering, and
  documented brand bias means users click recognized brands more —
  reinforces (with a named algorithm) the wiki's existing CTR-related
  findings without directly overlapping any specific existing page.
- **44.19% of searches are brand-related** (SparkToro/Rand Fishkin) —
  a new, useful scale stat for prioritizing branded-search work.
- **Monosemanticity**: LLM systems weight the clarity of word/concept
  alignment within content context (i.e., how unambiguously a term maps
  to one meaning) alongside engagement and entity co-occurrence — a
  concept not previously named in the wiki, relevant to disambiguation-
  focused entity work.
- **Organizational point (not content tactics)**: the author argues SEO
  practitioners should read the brand's own "Brand Bible" and
  collaborate rather than work in a technical silo, framing ROI in
  business terms — a process/collaboration recommendation, not a
  technical SEO tactic, so not folded into the technical playbooks.
