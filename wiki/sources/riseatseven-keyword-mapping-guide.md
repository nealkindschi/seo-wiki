Lidia Infante, "The Beginner's Guide to Keyword Mapping" (Rise at Seven).

## Key takeaways

- **Keyword mapping** = pairing target keywords to their intended landing
  pages, so the right page ranks for the right (conversion-relevant)
  keyword — a prerequisite for internal-linking strategy, precise SEO
  measurement, stakeholder reporting, and fast diagnosis of ranking
  drops.
- **5-step build process**: (1) identify ~20 priority pages via 12-month
  GA organic-landing-page data (sessions/conversions/conversion rate,
  filtered to business-critical pages); (2) establish one seed keyword
  per page (from the URL, validated against GSC top queries, keyword
  tools, and PPC data); (3) expand to ~10 keyword variations per page
  via Google Ads search terms, GSC queries, and Ahrefs/Semrush
  clustering, checking SERP intent and ad density as a commercial-value
  signal; (4) build the map in a tracked spreadsheet, tagging keywords
  by group and rank-tracking them; (5) turn gaps into an optimization
  roadmap.
- **Three optimization-roadmap categories** the map surfaces: missing
  content (important keyword with no dedicated page), non-ranking
  content (needs technical audit / content relevancy / internal-linking
  / competitive-benchmarking troubleshooting), and keyword
  cannibalization (consolidate pages, or reoptimize one for long-tail
  variants) — see [[entity-based-seo-implementation]] for the entity
  version of this cannibalization problem.
- Tools referenced: Ahrefs/Semrush (keyword clustering, rank tracking),
  SEOmonitor, Google Analytics, Google Search Console, Google Ads search
  term reports, Screaming Frog.
