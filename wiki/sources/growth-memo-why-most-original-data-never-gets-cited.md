# Why Most Original Data Never Gets Cited (Growth Memo)

**Citation:** Indig, Kevin and Johnson, Amanda. "Why Most Original Data
Never Gets Cited." Growth Memo. Published 2026-07-06.
https://www.growth-memo.com/p/why-most-original-data-never-gets
(Underlying dataset: Gauge, 301 cited pages / 1,075 citations / 7
verticals.)

## Key takeaways

- Original/proprietary data alone rarely earns AI citations — the
  deciding factor is *format*: whether it's packaged as a benchmark
  that directly answers "which option is best?" on measurable criteria.
- Primary research is rare (only 8 of 301 cited pages, 2.7%) but
  disproportionately citation-dense: those 8 pages earned 8.4% of all
  citations, averaging 11.3 citations each vs. 3.4 for other pages —
  "3.3x as citation-dense."
- Benchmarks dominate primary-research citations: 75 of 90 came from
  cloud data-warehouse benchmarks, with a single Fivetran benchmark
  page capturing 44 citations alone (its two benchmark pages together =
  58 of 90).
- **Topic-dependent**: benchmark-driven citation clustering only
  appears in topics with a clear, measurable comparison framework
  (speed/cost/latency/performance — warehouses, crypto/Solana). Topics
  without a natural comparison axis (B2B SaaS/CRM, Education/TEFL,
  Product Analytics) produced zero cited primary research.
- **Seven characteristics of a durable, citation-earning benchmark**
  (from the 2022 Fivetran case, still earning citations 4 years later):
  direct named-item comparison framing, first-party (not synthetic)
  testing data explicitly labeled as such, transparent step-by-step
  methodology, AI-retrievable heading structure, linked/verifiable
  source data, visible credibility signals (dated corrections,
  acknowledged limits), and a stable canonical URL since publication.
- **Structural recommendations**: lead with the comparison result in
  the first 30% of the page; box the methodology (sample, timeframe,
  measurement approach, confidence); frame explicitly as a comparison
  with tables of named options; keep the URL stable — 64 of 365 cited
  URLs in the dataset were dead/redirected, taking 203 citations
  offline.
- **Why most proprietary data fails to get cited**: brands have the
  data but bury it in narrative, gate it behind lead-gen forms, change
  URLs over time, or omit methodology — the data exists, the
  citation-ready packaging doesn't.
- Positions this as an "open opportunity": companies sitting on
  proprietary product/usage/pricing data can package it as buyer-facing
  comparisons in categories that currently lack a clean benchmark
  source.
