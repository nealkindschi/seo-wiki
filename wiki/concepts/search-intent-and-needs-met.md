# Search Intent and Needs Met

Google's official taxonomy for query intent and how human raters judge
whether a result satisfies it, per
[[google-search-quality-evaluator-guidelines-2025-09]]. Companion
framework to [[e-e-a-t-and-page-quality]] — Page Quality judges the page
itself, Needs Met judges whether a given result actually satisfies a
given query's intent.

## Query intent taxonomy

- **Know query**: the user wants to find information or explore a
  topic. Most informational queries are plain Know queries — broad,
  with no single correct short answer (e.g., [barack obama]: could mean
  biography, books, social media, etc.).
- **Know Simple query**: a special, narrower case — the query seeks one
  specific, correct, complete answer that fits in 1-2 sentences or a
  short list (e.g., [barack obama height], [how tall is obama]). Most
  queries are *not* Know Simple — broad/complex/ambiguous/controversial
  informational queries, queries with no definitive right answer, or
  queries seeking personal opinions/inspiration are all plain Know
  queries instead.
- **Do query**: the user wants to accomplish a goal or engage in an
  activity.
- **Website query**: the user is looking for a specific website or
  webpage.
- **Visit-in-person query**: the user wants to find a specific business/
  organization, or a category of businesses, to visit physically.

**AEO relevance**: Know Simple queries are exactly the kind of query
that gets pulled into short, direct-answer treatments (featured
snippets, answer boxes, and — by extension — the kind of concise,
single-fact answers generative engines are best positioned to lift
directly out of content). Structuring content so a Know-Simple-style
question has one clear, complete, correctly-stated 1-2 sentence answer
is a concrete, low-effort AEO tactic. See
[[geo-content-optimization-tactics]].

## Needs Met scale

How raters judge whether a specific result satisfies a specific query's
intent, from best to worst:

- **Fully Meets (FullyM)**: a special category only for queries with
  clear intent to find one specific result, matched by that exact
  result.
- **Highly Meets (HM)**: very helpful for any dominant, common, or
  reasonable minor interpretation of the query.
- **Moderately Meets (MM)**: helpful for any dominant, common, or
  reasonable minor interpretation.
- **Slightly Meets (SM)**: less helpful for a dominant/common
  interpretation, OR helpful only for an unlikely minor interpretation.
- **Fails to Meet (FailsM)**: completely fails to meet the needs of
  almost all users — off-topic, or addresses a no-chance interpretation
  of the query.

Needs Met and Page Quality are related but distinct: a high-quality page
can still fail to meet a specific query's need if it's simply not what
that query's users are looking for, and a lower-quality page can still
technically "meet" a need if nothing better is available for that
specific interpretation.
